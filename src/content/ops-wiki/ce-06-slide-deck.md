# Slide Deck — Two-Stage Approach

## Overview

There are two decks, not one. Each serves a specific stage of the sales process.

1. **The Initial Audit Deck** — Short, sharp, used in Stage 1 to create urgency
2. **The Deep Dive Deck** — Full analysis, used in Stage 2 to close

Both are generated using data from the Conversion Matrix and Revenue Projector.

---

## Deck 1: The Initial Audit (5-6 slides)

**Purpose:** Show the prospect their revenue gaps exist. Create urgency. Get buy-in for the deep dive.

**When:** Sent after the Stage 1 lightweight audit (public data only)

### Slide 1: Cover
- Ecom Landers logo (subtle)
- "[Brand Name] — Conversion Quick-Scan"
- Date
- "Prepared by Ecom Landers"

### Slide 2: What We Looked At
- "We ran your post-click experience through our 8-layer conversion framework"
- Screenshots of their key pages (homepage, top PDP, cart)
- Simple visual — show we actually looked at their site

### Slide 3: Where We See Gaps
- Quick Conversion Matrix scores (traffic light: red/amber/green)
- Top 3 problem areas called out with one-line explanations
- Keep it high-level — enough to create curiosity, not enough to self-solve

### Slide 4: The Estimated Revenue Gap
- Rough revenue projection based on public traffic data
- "Based on estimated traffic of ~X sessions/mo, we believe your CVR gap represents £X-X/mo in recoverable revenue"
- Caveat: "This is an estimate — with analytics access we can confirm the exact numbers"

### Slide 5: What We'd Recommend
- "A deeper analysis with your actual data to validate these findings"
- What we need: GA4 access, Shopify analytics, 30-minute call
- What they get: Full scored audit, exact revenue projection, 90-day roadmap draft
- "No commitment — if the numbers don't justify a partnership, we'll tell you"

### Slide 6: About Us (Optional)
- Brief credentials
- Key metric: "5,000+ pages built, average 1.5% CVR lift in 90 days"
- 1-2 mini case studies relevant to their vertical

**Design notes:** Light, clean, their branding prominent (our logo small). Data-light since we're working with estimates. The goal is curiosity, not overwhelm.

## Deck 2: The Deep Dive (10-12 slides)

**Purpose:** Present the full analysis with exact revenue projections. Close the partnership.

**When:** Presented on the Stage 2 call (after analytics access)

### Slide 1: Cover
- Ecom Landers logo
- "[Brand Name] — Conversion Engine Analysis"
- Date

### Slide 2: The Problem
- "Your ad spend is working. Your post-click experience isn't."
- Their actual monthly traffic number (from GA4)
- Their actual CVR vs industry benchmark
- Visual: funnel diagram with leak points highlighted in red

### Slide 3: Your Funnel, Scored
- Full Conversion Matrix scores by layer (8 layers)
- Red/amber/green visual for each
- Each layer has a one-line diagnosis
- Top 3 problem areas prominently called out

### Slide 4: The Numbers
- Revenue Projector output — exact numbers from their data
- Monthly gap figure (prominent, large font)
- Annual gap figure
- Breakeven calculation: "The partnership pays for itself at a 0.X% CVR lift"
- "This is revenue your traffic is already generating — you're losing it after the click"

### Slide 5: Where the Money Leaks (Detail)
- Deep dive into the top 3 problem layers
- Their actual screenshots with annotated issues
- Data points from their analytics (bounce rates, drop-offs, device splits)
- Competitor comparison: "Here's what [competitor] does on the same layer"

### Slide 6: The Ad Spend Multiplier
- Their current CPA vs potential CPA
- "Same ad spend, X% more customers"
- This slide resonates with founders who feel ROAS pressure

### Slide 7: What We'd Fix First
- 90-day roadmap preview (Month 1 / Month 2 / Month 3)
- Specific conversion assets identified (not generic — "your PDP hero section needs X")
- Before/after examples from similar brands we've worked with
- Expected impact per change

### Slide 8: How We Work
- The partnership model explained visually
- Month 1: Deep Audit + Roadmap + Quick Wins
- Month 2+: Build → Test → Measure → Compound
- "We own the roadmap. You approve the direction. Results compound monthly."
- "Not a vendor. Your conversion partner."

### Slide 9: The Offer
- Core Partnership: £X/mo (primary — make this feel like the obvious choice)
- Pro Partnership: £X/mo (anchor — makes Core feel accessible)
- What's included in each (keep concise — bullet points, not paragraphs)
- Breakeven reminder: "Pays for itself at a 0.X% CVR lift"

### Slide 10: The ROI Math
- Simple table: retainer cost vs projected revenue recovered
- Monthly ROI multiple (e.g., "9-18x return based on conservative 1-2% CVR lift")
- Daily cost of inaction: "Every day without this costs roughly £X"

### Slide 11: Case Studies
- 2-3 relevant case studies (same vertical or similar scale)
- Format: Before metric → After metric → Timeframe → What we did
- Keep each to 3-4 lines maximum

### Slide 12: Next Steps
- "Start date: [date]"
- "Week 1: Full system audit and 90-day roadmap"
- "First conversion assets live within 3 weeks"
- Contact details and booking link

## Design Principles

Both decks follow the same design rules:

- **Black and white, minimal** — let the numbers do the talking
- **No stock photography** — use their own site screenshots and data
- **Data-heavy, copy-light** — every slide has a number that matters
- **Their brand prominent, ours subtle** — this is about them, not us
- **One point per slide** — don't stack multiple ideas
- **Mobile-friendly** — they'll review on their phone before the call

## Delivery

- **Initial Audit Deck:** Sent as PDF via DM or email. No call required — it's a warm-up. Include a short Loom walking through the key findings (2-3 minutes).
- **Deep Dive Deck:** Presented live on a screen-share call. Never sent cold — the narrative matters as much as the data. Share the PDF after the call for them to review with their team.
