# Sales Process — Two-Stage Audit Close

## The System

The sale happens in two stages. Each stage has a specific job. The first warms them up with proof there's a problem. The second shows them exactly how much it's costing them and presents the partnership as the obvious fix.

**No cold pitching.** We never pitch the retainer on a first call. We earn the right to pitch by showing them their own data first.

## Stage 1: The Initial Audit (Warm-Up)

### Goal
Show the prospect their revenue gaps exist. Create urgency without selling. Get buy-in to run a deeper analysis.

### How It Happens
This can be triggered multiple ways:
- Inbound from content (X, LinkedIn, audit funnel page)
- DM conversation where they ask about CRO
- Referral from existing client
- Cold outreach with a personalised audit snippet

### The Initial Audit Itself
A lightweight, public-data-only audit that takes 20-30 minutes to produce:

1. **Site walkthrough** — Screenshot their homepage, top PDPs, cart, checkout. Note obvious conversion leaks.
2. **Traffic estimate** — SimilarWeb for traffic volume, sources, device split.
3. **Tech stack** — BuiltWith scan (Shopify Plus? Recharge? Klaviyo? What apps?).
4. **Quick conversion matrix** — Score their key funnel layers based on observation only (no analytics access yet).
5. **Revenue gap estimate** — Traffic × CVR gap × AOV. Even a rough estimate is powerful.

### Delivery Format
Send it as a short Loom or a one-page summary. Keep it visual — screenshots with annotations, not paragraphs of text. The message:

> "Ran your site through our conversion framework. You're strong on [X] but there are some clear gaps at [Y] and [Z] that are likely costing you £[amount]/mo. Happy to dig deeper if you're interested — we'd need analytics access to give you exact numbers."

### Key Phrases
- "We only work with brands where the maths works"
- "If your post-click experience is already optimised, we'll tell you"
- "We're not pitching — we're diagnosing"
- "I ran this because I saw [specific thing] and wanted to check if the gap was real"

### What This Achieves
- **Proof, not claims.** You've shown them their own leaks before asking for anything.
- **Authority.** They see a level of preparation that immediately separates you from every other agency.
- **Curiosity.** The rough revenue number creates urgency to see the full picture.
- **Permission.** They invite you to go deeper — you're not pushing.

## Stage 2: The Deep Dive (The Close)

### Goal
Present the full analysis with exact revenue projections. Close the partnership.

### Between Stage 1 and Stage 2
Once they've given analytics access (GA4, Shopify, heatmaps), this is where the system does the heavy lifting:

1. **Full Conversion Matrix score** — Every funnel layer scored with real data, not estimates. Drop-off rates, device splits, page-level performance.
2. **Revenue Projector calculation** — Exact traffic × CVR gap × AOV. This gives the revenue gap and breakeven number.
3. **Roadmap draft** — Top 3-5 conversion assets to build, sequenced by impact.
4. **Generate the deck** — Branded presentation with their scores, gap, roadmap preview, and pricing.

> **The end state:** Analytics access → score → calculate → generate deck. All before Stage 2. The prospect sees a level of preparation no other agency delivers.

### The Deep Dive Call (45-60 mins)

**Structure:**
1. **Recap** — "Here's what we found in the initial audit. Now we've had access to your actual data." (2 mins)
2. **Conversion Matrix walkthrough** — Layer by layer, show where they're leaking. Use their own screenshots and data. (10-15 mins)
3. **Revenue projection** — "Based on your actual traffic and CVR, closing this gap = £X/mo in recovered revenue." (5 mins)
4. **The roadmap** — "Here's what we'd fix first and why. This isn't a guess — it's prioritised by revenue impact." (10 mins)
5. **The offer** — "Our partnership is £X/mo. Based on these numbers, it pays for itself at a 0.X% CVR lift. Most of our clients see 1-2% in the first 90 days." (5 mins)
6. **Objection handling and next steps** (10 mins)

### The Proof vs Evidence Framework

This is the key distinction that makes the close work:

- **Evidence** = what we observe (their site has problems)
- **Proof** = what the data confirms (their site is losing £X/mo)

Stage 1 gives evidence. Stage 2 gives proof. By the time you present the offer, you've already proved the ROI mathematically. The retainer isn't a cost — it's the cheapest way to capture revenue they're already losing.

Use the conversion matrix to show this visually:
- Calculate their traffic and ad spend
- Show their sessions and current CVR
- Project what happens when CVR improves by 1%, 2%, 3%
- Frame it: "You're currently missing £300-500K in annual revenue. Our partnership recovers a fraction of that in Month 1."

## Pre-Sales Research Checklist

Before the Deep Dive call, confirm you have:

- [ ] GA4 access and traffic data pulled
- [ ] Shopify analytics / CVR data confirmed
- [ ] AOV calculated from actual data
- [ ] Ad spend estimate (from Meta Ad Library activity or client confirmation)
- [ ] Tech stack documented (BuiltWith — Shopify Plus? Key apps?)
- [ ] Full Conversion Matrix scored with real data
- [ ] Revenue gap calculated and ready (with breakeven number)
- [ ] 90-day roadmap drafted (top 3-5 priorities)
- [ ] Branded deck generated and reviewed
- [ ] 2-3 case study references relevant to their vertical
- [ ] Competitor landscape noted (who's doing post-click well in their space)

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We already have an agency" | "Great — are they doing post-click? Most agencies stop at the ad or the email. We start where they stop. We're the conversion layer — just like you have an email agency and an ad agency, this is your CRO team." |
| "We can't commit to a retainer" | "I understand. But one-off pages don't compound. You test, learn, iterate — that's where the ROI lives. The brands seeing 2-3% CVR lifts aren't doing it with isolated projects." |
| "It's too expensive" | "Your current CVR gap is costing you £X/mo in lost revenue. The retainer is a fraction of that. At a 0.1% lift it pays for itself — we average 1-2% in 90 days." |
| "We want to see results first" | "That's exactly what the first 90 days deliver. Audit, roadmap, first builds, first tests. If the numbers don't move, we'll have an honest conversation." |
| "Can we start smaller?" | "We can — but a half-effort gets half-results. The reason this works is we own the full post-click experience, not just one page. Splitting it up is like hiring half a surgeon." |
| "We've used agencies before" | "That's exactly the problem. Every time you switch vendors you lose 3 months of momentum. New onboarding, new context, back to zero. Our partnership compounds — Month 3 builds on Month 2, every test informs the next build. That visibility gap from jumping between vendors is what's killing your progress." |
| "We'll do it in-house" | "A senior CRO hire is £60-80K/year plus tools, training, and 6 months to ramp. You're getting a full team — strategist, designer, developer, QA — with 5,000+ builds behind us. For a fraction of one hire." |
| "We need to think about it" | "Of course. While you think, your CVR gap costs roughly £X every day. Happy to reconnect next week — but the roadmap we've built is time-sensitive because your competitive landscape moves fast." |

## Proposal Delivery — Offer Engine

After the Deep Dive call, the proposal is the artefact that confirms the shape of the engagement. Use the **Offer Engine** tool in Launchpad to draft it instead of writing from scratch — the template is modelled on the Yorkshire Dental Suite format (Conversion Engine retainer + scoped pilot).

### When to use it
- Immediately after the Deep Dive close call, while the conversation is fresh
- Any prospect we've committed to sending a formal SOW to
- Not for the Initial Audit stage — those stay as Looms / one-pagers

### How it works
1. Open `/tools/offer-engine` (link above).
2. Paste the call notes or transcript — Claude only pulls stats from what you paste.
3. Fill the offer inputs — retainer price, pilot scope, pilot price.
4. Generate. The output lives at `/proposal/[brand-slug]` as a shareable page.
5. Any number Claude couldn't verify from the notes stays as `[needs input]` so you spot it before sending.

### Before sending
- Read it end-to-end. The generator is an accelerator, not an autopilot.
- Verify every stat in Section 01 against the notes or the Conversion Matrix output.
- Confirm the pilot scope matches what was agreed on the call.
- If the numbers are wrong, regenerate with tighter notes rather than patching by hand.
