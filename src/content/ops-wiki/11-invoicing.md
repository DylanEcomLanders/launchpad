# Invoicing & Financial Ops

## Invoice Timing

| Scenario | When to Invoice |
|----------|----------------|
| One-off project (50/50) | 50% on signing, 50% on delivery |
| One-off project (milestone) | Per agreed milestones (e.g., 30% start, 40% design approval, 30% go-live) |
| Retainer | 1st of each month, in advance |
| CRO Audit | 100% upfront before work begins |
| Rush/priority work | 100% upfront |

> **Never start work without the first invoice paid.** No exceptions. Not for "big clients", not for "we'll pay next week." Payment in = work starts.

## Payment Terms

- **Standard terms:** Net 14 (payment due within 14 days of invoice date)
- **Retainer terms:** Due on receipt (since it's for the coming month's work)
- **Accepted methods:** Bank transfer, Stripe (card payments carry a 2.9% processing fee, passed to client)

### Payment Terms in Proposals
Include this exact language in every proposal and contract:

```
Payment Terms: Net 14 from invoice date. Late payments incur a 
5% late fee after 14 days and an additional 2% per week thereafter. 
Work will be paused on any account with an overdue invoice exceeding 
21 days.
```

## Invoice Template

Every invoice must include:

### Header
- Ecom Landers logo and business details
- Invoice number (format: `EL-[YEAR]-[SEQUENTIAL]`, e.g., `EL-2026-047`)
- Invoice date
- Due date
- Client name and billing address

### Line Items

| Description | Qty | Rate | Total |
|-------------|-----|------|-------|
| Landing Page Design — Hydra Supplements | 1 | $3,500 | $3,500 |
| Development Build — Landing Page | 1 | $4,000 | $4,000 |

- Be specific in descriptions — the client should recognise exactly what they're paying for
- Don't use vague descriptions like "Design Services" or "Consulting"
- For retainers, list the month: "Monthly Retainer — April 2026"

### Footer
- Subtotal
- Tax (if applicable)
- Total due
- Payment methods and bank details
- Late payment terms

## Expense Tracking

### What to Track
- Software subscriptions (Figma, analytics tools, hosting)
- Contractor payments (freelance designers, developers)
- Stock photography or assets purchased for client work
- Domain registrations (if purchased on behalf of clients)

### Rules
- Client-specific expenses (stock photos bought for their project, premium fonts licensed for their brand) are billed to the client as a line item on their invoice
- Agency overhead (Figma, general tools) is not billed to clients
- Log every expense on the day it occurs — don't batch at month end
- Keep receipts for everything (digital is fine, use a shared folder)

## Retainer Billing

### Structure
- Billed monthly in advance on the 1st
- Hours are specified in the contract (e.g., 20 hours/month)
- Unused hours do not roll over (unless the contract explicitly states otherwise)
- Overage hours are billed at the standard hourly rate on a separate invoice at month end

### Monthly Retainer Invoice Flow
1. **1st of month:** Send retainer invoice for the coming month
2. **Throughout month:** Track hours against tasks
3. **End of month:** Review hours logged
4. **If overage:** Send a separate overage invoice with a breakdown of extra hours
5. **With monthly report:** Include hours summary (hours used, hours remaining, any overage)

### Hours Tracking
- Log hours daily, not weekly (memory is unreliable after 5 days)
- Log against specific tasks, not "general work"
- Minimum billing increment: 15 minutes
- Travel time and meeting time are billable unless stated otherwise

## One-Off Project Billing

### 50/50 Split (Standard)
- **Invoice 1:** 50% on contract signing (before any work begins)
- **Invoice 2:** 50% on project delivery (before go-live)

### Milestone Split (Larger Projects)
- **Invoice 1:** 30% on contract signing
- **Invoice 2:** 40% on design approval
- **Invoice 3:** 30% on go-live

### When to Use Which
- Projects under $10,000: 50/50
- Projects $10,000-25,000: Milestone (3 payments)
- Projects over $25,000: Milestone (4+ payments, monthly or per phase)

> **The final payment must be received before the theme is published live.** This is non-negotiable. Don't hand over the keys until the bill is paid.

## Late Payment Process

### Day 1 (Invoice Due Date)
- Automated payment reminder sent
- No action needed from you

### Day 7 (1 week overdue)
- Personal email from the account lead:
  ```
  Hi [Name],
  
  Just a quick note — invoice [number] was due on [date] and 
  we haven't received payment yet. Could you check on this?
  
  Happy to answer any questions about the invoice.
  ```

### Day 14 (2 weeks overdue)
- Phone call or direct message
- Firm but professional:
  ```
  Hi [Name],
  
  Invoice [number] is now 14 days overdue. Per our agreement, 
  a 5% late fee has been applied.
  
  Please arrange payment within the next 48 hours. If there's 
  an issue with the invoice, let's discuss it today.
  ```

### Day 21 (3 weeks overdue)
- **All work paused** on the account
- Formal email:
  ```
  Hi [Name],
  
  As per our contract terms, we've paused all work on your 
  account due to the outstanding invoice [number], which is 
  now 21 days overdue.
  
  We'll resume work immediately upon receipt of payment. 
  Please note that additional late fees of 2% per week apply 
  to the outstanding balance.
  ```

### Day 30+ (1 month overdue)
- Escalate to agency owner
- Consider formal demand letter
- Evaluate whether to continue the relationship

> **Late payment is a business problem, not a personal one.** Be direct, be professional, and follow the process. Don't let unpaid invoices slide because you feel awkward — that awkwardness costs the business real money.
