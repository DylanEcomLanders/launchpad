# Client Onboarding — Portal Setup

## Purpose

Every new client gets a branded portal from day one. This sets the tone for a professional, organised engagement and gives the client visibility into their project from the start. No client should ever have to ask "what's happening with my project?" — the portal answers that question 24/7.

## When

Immediately after the sale closes — within 24 hours. Do not wait for the kickoff call. The portal should be live before the first meeting so you can walk the client through it on the call.

## Onboarding Steps

### Step 1: Open Project Kickoff in Launchpad
- Navigate to the Project Kickoff tool in Launchpad
- Select "New Client" or "Existing Client" (returning clients get added to their existing portal)

### Step 2: Fill In Client & Project Details
- **Client name** (company name, not contact name)
- **Primary contact** (name, email, phone)
- **Project type** (Landing Page, Homepage Redesign, Collection Page, CRO Audit, Retainer)
- **Project value** (total contract value)
- **Start date** and **target delivery date**
- **Brief summary** (one-liner describing the project)

### Step 3: Add Deliverables
Create a deliverable for each key output:
- [ ] Wireframes
- [ ] High-Fidelity Design (Desktop)
- [ ] High-Fidelity Design (Mobile)
- [ ] Development Build
- [ ] QA & Testing
- [ ] Go-Live
- [ ] Post-Launch Review

Set estimated completion dates for each. These become the timeline the client sees.

### Step 4: Create Portal or Add to Existing
- For new clients: Create a new portal with the client's brand colour
- For returning clients: Add the new project to their existing portal
- Ensure the portal URL is clean and professional

### Step 5: Send Portal Link to Client
Send a welcome email with:
- Portal link
- Brief explanation of what they'll find there
- What to expect in the first week
- Contact details for their account lead

**Email template:**

```
Subject: Welcome to Ecom Landers — Your Project Portal

Hi [Name],

Welcome aboard! We're excited to get started on [project].

Your project portal is live here: [Portal URL]

In your portal you'll find:
- Your project timeline and milestones
- A deliverable tracker (updated as we progress)
- A central place to share feedback and approvals

Next steps:
1. Review the portal and let us know if anything looks off
2. We'll send over an access request for your Shopify store and analytics
3. Our kickoff call is scheduled for [date/time]

If you need anything before then, just reply to this email.

Looking forward to working together.

[Your name]
Ecom Landers
```

### Step 6: Schedule Kickoff Call
- Book a 30-minute kickoff call within 3-5 business days of signing
- Send a calendar invite with a clear agenda:
  - Introductions (if needed)
  - Walk through the portal and timeline
  - Review the brief and confirm scope
  - Discuss brand guidelines and assets needed
  - Agree on communication cadence
  - Align on access requirements (Shopify, GA4, etc.)

## What the Client Receives

| Deliverable | When |
|------------|------|
| Portal link | Within 24 hours of signing |
| Welcome email | Same day as portal |
| Access request list | Within 24 hours |
| Kickoff call | Within 3-5 business days |
| Project timeline in portal | Before kickoff call |
| First deliverable (usually wireframes) | Per agreed timeline |

## Access Request Checklist

Send this to the client alongside the portal link:

- [ ] Shopify admin access (staff account with theme and analytics permissions)
- [ ] Google Analytics 4 (Viewer access — send invite to team@ecomlanders.com)
- [ ] Google Search Console (if SEO is in scope)
- [ ] Brand assets (logo files, brand guidelines PDF, font files)
- [ ] Product photography (high-res originals, not screenshots)
- [ ] Existing copy document (if they have approved copy)
- [ ] Competitor references (3-5 brands they admire)
- [ ] Meta Business Suite access (if running paid social)

> **Do not start design work until you have brand assets and store access.** Chasing assets mid-project causes delays and frustration on both sides.

## Red Flags During Onboarding

Watch for these warning signs and address them immediately:

### Multiple Approval Stakeholders
- **Risk:** Conflicting feedback, slow approvals, revision loops
- **Action:** Identify a single decision-maker before the kickoff call. State clearly: "We need one person to give final approval on each deliverable. Who is that?"

### No Analytics Access
- **Risk:** Can't set baselines, can't measure results, can't justify the work
- **Action:** Make analytics access a prerequisite for starting. Frame it as: "We need this to measure the ROI of your investment with us"

### Vague Goals
- **Risk:** Scope creep, misaligned expectations, "this isn't what I had in mind"
- **Action:** Push for specific, measurable goals during kickoff. "Increase conversion rate" becomes "Increase landing page CVR from 1.8% to 2.5% within 60 days"

### Slow Communication
- **Risk:** Project timeline slips, team sits idle waiting for feedback
- **Action:** Set response time expectations upfront: "We'll need feedback within 2 business days to stay on timeline. Delays in feedback push the delivery date."

### "We'll Figure It Out as We Go"
- **Risk:** No brief = no direction = endless revisions
- **Action:** Do not start without a completed brief. Offer to help them fill it out on the kickoff call if needed, but get it documented.

> **Onboarding sets the tone for the entire engagement.** A well-run onboarding builds client confidence and prevents 80% of mid-project problems. Invest the time upfront.
