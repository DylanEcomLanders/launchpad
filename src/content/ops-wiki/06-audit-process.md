# CRO Audit Process

## When to Run an Audit

Audits are a core service offering and a primary lead generation tool. Run an audit in these scenarios:

- **New lead qualification** — free audit as a sales tool to demonstrate expertise and win the project
- **New client onboarding** — paid audit to establish a performance baseline before any design work
- **Retainer clients** — quarterly audit to identify optimisation opportunities
- **Post-launch** — 30 days after a landing page goes live, audit its performance
- **Performance decline** — when a client reports dropping conversion rates

> **A free audit should take no more than 2-3 hours.** A paid comprehensive audit takes 1-2 days. Scope accordingly.

## Data Collection

### Required Access (request from client before starting)
- [ ] Google Analytics 4 (Viewer access minimum)
- [ ] Shopify admin (Staff access or Analytics access)
- [ ] Heatmap tool (Hotjar, MS Clarity, or similar) — if installed
- [ ] Session recordings (same tool) — if available
- [ ] Google Search Console (if SEO is in scope)

### GA4 Data to Pull
- **Conversion funnel**: Landing page → Product view → ATC → Checkout → Purchase
- **Conversion rate** by device (desktop vs. mobile)
- **Bounce rate** and **exit rate** by page
- **Average session duration** and **pages per session**
- **Revenue per session**
- **Traffic sources** breakdown
- **Date range**: Last 30 days minimum, 90 days preferred

### Heatmap & Session Data
- Click heatmaps for the landing page (desktop + mobile)
- Scroll depth map (where do users stop scrolling?)
- Session recordings: watch 20-30 sessions minimum, split across:
  - Users who converted
  - Users who bounced
  - Mobile users specifically
- Note rage clicks, dead clicks, and confusion patterns

### Shopify Data
- Sales by traffic source
- Cart abandonment rate
- Average order value
- Top-selling products (to check if the landing page aligns)

## The 8-Layer Scoring Method

Score each layer 1-10. This creates a structured, repeatable audit framework.

### Layer 1: First Impression (Above the Fold)
- Does the headline communicate the value proposition in under 5 seconds?
- Is the primary CTA visible without scrolling on mobile?
- Does the hero image/video support the message?
- Is there visual clutter competing with the core message?

### Layer 2: Value Proposition Clarity
- Can a new visitor understand what's being sold and why it matters?
- Are benefits front and centre (not just features)?
- Is the unique selling proposition clear vs. competitors?

### Layer 3: Trust & Social Proof
- Are there reviews/testimonials visible?
- Is social proof placed near key decision points (not buried at the bottom)?
- Are trust badges, guarantees, and certifications present?
- Do the reviews feel authentic (photos, full names, specific details)?

### Layer 4: Visual Hierarchy & Layout
- Does the eye flow naturally down the page?
- Is there a clear content hierarchy (H1 → H2 → H3)?
- Are CTAs visually distinct from other elements?
- Is the page scannable (not a wall of text)?

### Layer 5: CTA Strategy
- Is there a clear primary CTA?
- Are CTAs repeated at appropriate intervals?
- Does the CTA copy communicate value (not just "Submit" or "Click Here")?
- Are there too many competing CTAs diluting focus?

### Layer 6: Objection Handling
- Does the page address common buying objections?
- Is shipping/return info easy to find?
- Is there an FAQ section for remaining questions?
- Are guarantees or risk-reversal elements present?

### Layer 7: Mobile Experience
- Is the mobile layout purpose-built (not just a squished desktop)?
- Do CTAs have adequate tap targets (minimum 44x44px)?
- Is text readable without zooming?
- Do forms work well on mobile (proper input types, autofill)?

### Layer 8: Page Performance
- LCP under 2.5 seconds?
- CLS under 0.1?
- No layout shifts caused by late-loading elements?
- Images optimised and lazy-loaded?

## Audit Report Structure

### 1. Executive Summary (1 page)
- Overall score out of 80
- Top 3 issues hurting conversions
- Estimated impact of fixing them (use ranges: "We estimate a 15-25% CVR improvement")
- Recommended next steps

### 2. Data Overview (1-2 pages)
- Current conversion metrics
- Traffic breakdown
- Device split performance
- Key funnel drop-off points

### 3. Layer-by-Layer Analysis (4-6 pages)
- Each layer gets its own section
- Include annotated screenshots showing the specific issues
- Rate severity: Critical / High / Medium / Low
- Provide specific, actionable recommendations (not vague advice)

### 4. Priority Roadmap (1 page)
- Quick wins (can fix in 1-2 days, high impact)
- Medium-term improvements (1-2 week projects)
- Strategic changes (require design/build work)

### 5. Appendix
- Raw data tables
- Full heatmap screenshots
- Session recording highlights (with timestamps)

## Presenting Findings to the Client

### Do
- Lead with the biggest opportunities, not the problems
- Frame everything as revenue impact: "This change could increase your CVR by X%"
- Use their own data — screenshots of their analytics, their heatmaps
- Have a clear next-steps proposal ready
- Be specific: "Change the hero CTA from 'Learn More' to 'Shop Now'" not "improve CTAs"

### Don't
- Be condescending about their current site
- Present 50 recommendations at once (overwhelm kills action)
- Use jargon without explaining it
- Present problems without solutions
- Promise specific numbers — use ranges and frame as estimates

### Presentation Format
- Loom video walkthrough (15-20 minutes max) + PDF report
- For high-value clients, do it live on a call
- Always send the report before the call so they can review

## Timeline

| Audit Type | Data Collection | Analysis | Report | Total |
|-----------|----------------|----------|--------|-------|
| Free (lead gen) | 1 hour | 1 hour | 1 hour | 3 hours |
| Standard (paid) | 2-3 hours | 4-6 hours | 3-4 hours | 2 days |
| Comprehensive (retainer) | 4-6 hours | 6-8 hours | 4-6 hours | 3 days |

> **Free audits are a sales tool.** They should be impressive enough to win the project but not so thorough that you've given away all the value. Show what's wrong and hint at how to fix it — the detailed roadmap is part of the paid engagement.
