# Client Communication & Updates

## Communication Channels

Use the right channel for the right message. Don't send a formal project update via Slack, and don't send a quick question via email.

| Channel | Use For | Response Time |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Slack / Direct Message** | Quick questions, daily check-ins, informal updates, sharing links | Same business day (within 4 hours) |
| **Email** | Formal updates, deliverable submissions, approvals, invoices, contracts | Same business day |
| **Loom** | Design walkthroughs, build previews, explaining complex changes, weekly updates | N/A (async, no response expected) |
| **Video Call** | Kickoff meetings, strategy sessions, difficult conversations, monthly reviews | Scheduled in advance |
| **Phone** | Urgent issues only (site down, go-live problem) | Immediate |

### Rules
- **Default to async.** Don't schedule a meeting for something a Loom or email can handle
- **One channel per topic.** Don't start a conversation in Slack, continue it in email, and finish it on a call. Pick a channel and keep the thread there
- **Confirm decisions in writing.** If a client verbally approves something on a call, follow up with an email: "Confirming you've approved [X] — we'll proceed with [next step]"

## Response Time Standards

| Priority | Response Time | Examples |
|----------|--------------|---------|
| Urgent | Within 1 hour during business hours | Site broken, checkout down, go-live issue |
| High | Within 4 hours during business hours | Client feedback, approval request, blocker question |
| Standard | Same business day | General questions, status updates, non-urgent requests |
| Low | Within 2 business days | Future planning, nice-to-have requests, FYI messages |

### Business Hours
Our business hours are Monday-Friday, 9am-6pm (local time). Messages received outside of these hours will be responded to the next business day.

> **If you can't meet a response time, acknowledge receipt.** "Got your message — I'll have a full answer for you by [time]" is always better than silence.

## Weekly Updates Format

Send every Friday by 4pm. Every active client gets one, no exceptions.

### Template

```
Subject: [Client Name] — Weekly Update — [Date]

Hi [Name],

Here's your weekly update for [project name]:

COMPLETED THIS WEEK
- [Deliverable/task completed]
- [Deliverable/task completed]

IN PROGRESS
- [What's currently being worked on] — expected completion [date]
- [What's currently being worked on] — expected completion [date]

WAITING ON YOU
- [Any items needing client input/approval — be specific]

NEXT WEEK
- [What we'll focus on next week]

[Optional: Loom link for visual walkthrough]

Let me know if you have any questions.

[Your name]
```

### Rules for Weekly Updates
- Keep it scannable — bullet points, not paragraphs
- Be specific about dates — "early next week" is vague, "by Tuesday" is clear
- Always include a "Waiting On You" section if applicable — this creates gentle accountability
- If there's nothing meaningful to report, still send the update. "No changes this week — we're waiting on [X] before we can proceed" is a valid update
- Attach a Loom if anything visual has changed

## Sharing Bad News

Bad news doesn't get better with age. The longer you wait, the worse it gets.

### The Formula
1. **State the issue clearly.** No burying it in jargon or vague language
2. **Explain the impact.** What does this mean for the timeline/budget/deliverable?
3. **Present your plan.** What are you doing to fix it? When will it be resolved?
4. **Take ownership.** If it's your fault, say so. Don't blame the client, don't blame tools, don't make excuses

### Example: Missed Deadline

**Bad:**
```
Hi [Name], we're still working on the landing page. 
Should be done soon. Sorry for the delay!
```

**Good:**
```
Hi [Name],

The landing page build is running 2 days behind schedule. 
The cause was a technical issue with the theme's cart integration 
that required a rebuild of the checkout flow section.

Here's our updated plan:
- Build completion: Wednesday (was Monday)
- Internal QA: Thursday
- Preview link to you: Friday

This doesn't impact the go-live date — we've compressed the 
QA window to absorb the delay.

I should have flagged this earlier. Going forward, I'll 
raise timeline risks the moment I see them.
```

### What NOT to Do
- Don't hide behind email when a call would be better (especially for big issues)
- Don't blame the client ("well, you took 5 days to send us the copy...")
- Don't minimise the issue ("it's just a small delay") when it's actually significant
- Don't present the problem without a solution

## Meeting Etiquette

### Before the Meeting
- Send an agenda at least 24 hours before
- Share any documents or links they need to review beforehand
- Confirm the meeting 2 hours before (a quick "See you at 2pm" message)

### During the Meeting
- Start on time. If the client is late, wait 5 minutes then send a "just checking if you're still available" message
- Stick to the agenda. If new topics come up, note them for follow-up
- Take notes. Assign someone to capture action items
- End 5 minutes early to summarise next steps

### After the Meeting
- Send a follow-up email within 2 hours with:
  - Summary of decisions made
  - Action items (who, what, by when)
  - Next meeting date (if applicable)

### Meeting Duration Guidelines
- **Quick sync:** 15 minutes
- **Weekly check-in:** 30 minutes
- **Design review:** 30-45 minutes
- **Strategy/kickoff:** 45-60 minutes
- **Anything over 60 minutes:** Needs a very good reason or should be split into two sessions

## Client Feedback Process

### How to Request Feedback

**Do:**
- Be specific about what you need feedback on: "Please review the hero section layout and CTA placement"
- Set a deadline: "We'd love your feedback by Wednesday so we can stay on timeline"
- Provide context: "This is round 1 of 2 included revision rounds"
- Use Loom to walk through the design before asking for feedback

**Don't:**
- Send a bare Figma link with "thoughts?"
- Ask for open-ended feedback without guardrails
- Let feedback trickle in over 2 weeks

### How to Handle Feedback

1. **Acknowledge receipt** — "Got your feedback, reviewing it now"
2. **Consolidate** — If feedback comes from multiple people, combine it into one list and confirm with the primary decision-maker
3. **Clarify before acting** — If feedback is vague ("make it pop"), ask a specific follow-up ("Do you mean brighter colours, larger typography, or more contrast?")
4. **Push back when needed** — If feedback would hurt the page's performance or conversion potential, explain why professionally (see `15-scope-management.md`)
5. **Confirm implementation** — Before delivering revisions, summarise what you're changing: "Based on your feedback, we'll update X, Y, and Z. Sound right?"

> **The goal of the feedback process is to reach approval in the fewest rounds possible.** Clear communication on both sides is the shortcut.
