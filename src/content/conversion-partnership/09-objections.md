# Objection Handling

Ten objections you'll hear most often, with the response we lead with. The cheat sheet at `/internal/cheatsheet/conversion-engine` has copy-to-clipboard buttons on each one for live calls.

## "We already have a CRO agency."

Most CRO agencies audit and recommend — we audit, build, and ship from one team. If yours is moving CVR every month, keep them. If they're sending 47-page reports and waiting on your dev team, that's the gap we fill.

## "We have an in-house designer/dev."

Good — that keeps cost down. We work two ways: hand over Figma + section specs for them to build, or run end-to-end and let them focus on product and brand. Most clients pick the second after one cycle.

## "It's too expensive."

The £8K retainer pays back at a 0.1% CVR lift on £800K monthly revenue. If your traffic and revenue numbers don't make those maths work, we're not the right fit and we'll tell you. Walk us through the numbers first — the gap is usually bigger than the retainer.

## "Why not a freelancer?"

A freelancer gives you pages. We give you a roadmap, a test loop, and revenue attribution. The £500 page on a brand with no testing programme doesn't compound — that's the difference.

## "How is this different from [competitor]?"

Most competitors split into two camps — strategy-only or build-only. We do both from the same team, so there's no handoff gap and the strategist who scoped the test is the one reviewing the result.

## "We tried CRO before and it didn't work."

One page is a guess. A programme is a process of elimination. Send us what was tested — usually the issue is offer mismatch, wrong traffic source, or a single shot with no iteration.

## "Can you guarantee results?"

No. Anyone guaranteeing CVR lift is lying or charging for the risk. We guarantee the system: a roadmap, monthly builds, tracked tests, and revenue attribution. If 90 days in the numbers haven't moved, we have an honest conversation.

## "Why a retainer and not project-based?"

One-off projects don't compound. The first build teaches you something — a retainer means we apply that on the next build instead of starting cold. We do projects, but the ROI only stacks on retainer.

## "We're not ready, we want to wait until [event]."

Every month you wait costs the CVR gap × revenue. If a replatform or brand refresh is genuinely blocking, we'll wait. If it's "Q4" or "after the launch" — that's when you need it most, not least.

## "Can we start with just one funnel?"

That's the standard £8K retainer. The full system focused on one funnel. If it lifts CVR there, expand scope from there — we don't tier or upsell, we just price the bigger scope.

---

## When to walk away

If the maths genuinely doesn't work — traffic too low, revenue too low, CVR already strong — they're not our client. Don't discount. Say it clearly:

> "The partnership is designed for brands where the revenue gap justifies the investment. Based on your numbers, it might not make sense right now. When you're doing [X traffic/revenue], let's revisit."

Walking away early protects the offer's premium positioning. Discounted retainers signal a vendor relationship — and vendor relationships churn.
