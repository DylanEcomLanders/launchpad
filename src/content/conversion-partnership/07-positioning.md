# Positioning & Language Guide

## Core Positioning Statement

"Ecom Landers is the conversion partner for DTC brands. We don't build pages — we build revenue systems."

## The Conversion Layer Concept

This is our primary positioning angle. Frame it every time:

> Every serious DTC brand has three layers:
> 1. **Ad layer** — Meta agency, Google agency, TikTok agency (drives traffic)
> 2. **Email layer** — Klaviyo agency (captures and nurtures)
> 3. **Conversion layer** — **Us** (turns traffic into revenue)
>
> Most brands have layers 1 and 2 dialled. Layer 3 is either missing or run by a freelancer doing one-off page builds. That's the gap. We are the conversion layer.

This positions us as essential infrastructure, not optional. You wouldn't run a DTC brand without an ad agency. You shouldn't run one without a conversion partner.

## The Revenue Anchor

Stop selling what's included. Sell what the CVR gap is costing them.

> Brand doing £800K/mo. We lift site-wide CVR by 2%. That's £16K/mo in recovered revenue. The partnership is £9K. They're paying half the value we deliver — and it compounds every month.

Frame every pricing conversation around this maths:
- **Their number** — monthly revenue and traffic (from SimilarWeb or conversation)
- **The gap** — current CVR vs benchmark (from conversion matrix)
- **The cost of doing nothing** — revenue gap × 12 months
- **The partnership** — a fraction of the recovered revenue

The retainer isn't an expense. It's the cheapest way to capture revenue they're already earning but losing after the click.

## The Premium Price Language

At £8K+/mo, the language needs to match the positioning:

**Don't apologise for the price. Contextualise it.**

- "That's less than one senior CRO hire, and you get the full team."
- "Your ad spend is £50K/mo. This is 16% of that — and it makes the other 84% work harder."
- "At a 0.1% CVR lift this pays for itself. We average 1-2% in 90 days."
- "Every month you delay costs roughly £X in lost revenue."

**Don't discount. Reframe.**

If someone pushes on price, don't drop it. Show the maths again. If the maths doesn't justify the retainer for their brand, they're not the right client — walk away. The brands where the maths works will pay it without negotiating.

But your site looks like 2019, your pages don't convert, and your customers don't come back. We're the bridge. We take the post-click experience from dropshipper-grade to brand-grade — and the CVR and AOV follow."

## Social Proof Framework

When sharing results:
- Lead with the metric: "2.1% → 4.3% CVR in 90 days"
- Follow with the context: "DTC supplements brand, £100K/mo revenue"
- Close with the method: "3 landing page builds + checkout optimisation + post-purchase upsell"
- Never name the client unless they've explicitly approved it

## The Vendor-Jumping Problem

Most brands in our target range have used 2-3 agencies or freelancers in the last 18 months. Each time they switch:
- New onboarding, new context dump, new learning curve
- Previous work gets abandoned or half-implemented
- No compounding — every project starts from scratch
- They lose 3 months of momentum at every switch

This is the single biggest argument for a partnership model. We don't restart. We compound. Month 3 builds on Month 2. The data from the first test informs the next build. A new vendor can't do that — they're always starting cold.

**Use this on every sales call when they say "we've used agencies before."** It reframes the partnership as the solution to a problem they already feel deeply.
