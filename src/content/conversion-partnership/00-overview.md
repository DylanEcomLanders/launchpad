# Conversion Engine — The Offer

## The Positioning

We are not a vendor. We are not an agency. We are their **conversion partner**.

Vendors get briefed, build, and hand over. Agencies create reports and recommendations. A conversion partner owns the entire post-click revenue system — we diagnose, we strategise, we build, we test, we compound. The client doesn't brief individual pages. We control the roadmap.

**Think of us as the conversion layer** — just like brands have an email agency (Klaviyo) and an ad agency (Meta/Google), they need a conversion agency. We are that layer. The one that turns the traffic they're already paying for into revenue.

## Pricing

The offer has a single buy-in and a single anchor. **No tiers. No T1/T2. No Core/Pro.** One number per shape, always quoted together.

### £8K/mo — the buy-in

This is the deal. The number we quote. The full Conversion Engine in its standard shape.

**Includes:**
- Comprehensive conversion audit and revenue gap analysis (Month 1)
- 60–90 day visual strategy roadmap (Miro) — updated monthly
- Monthly page builds (landing pages, PDPs, cart/checkout optimisation)
- A/B testing programme with if/then hypothesis chains
- AOV optimisation (bundles, upsells, cross-sells, post-purchase offers)
- Post-purchase flow design (thank-you pages, email sequences, replenishment)
- Offer and positioning optimisation
- Monthly reporting with revenue attribution
- Dedicated Slack channel with the team

**Positioning:** "Your full conversion team for less than one senior hire."

### £12K/mo — the anchor

This exists so £8K feels like a steal. Always quote both — never quote £12K alone.

Same system, more of it: faster turnarounds (48h on design, 5 days on build), more resources, dedicated strategist on weekly calls, ad creative → landing page alignment, quarterly business reviews.

**When to lead with anchor:** brands scaling past 8 figures who need speed and depth, or where the £8K shape would constrain delivery.

### Larger scopes

Pricing scales upward from £8K naturally as scope grows — not via tier. If a prospect needs more than the anchor, we price the bigger scope as its own number. No upselling between fixed packages.

## Why This Model Works

Always quoting £8K alongside £12K makes the £8K number feel obvious — premium, but accessible. The anchor is doing the comparison work; the buy-in is doing the closing.

### Team-Led, Not Client-Led

This is the critical shift from our old model. The client does **not** brief individual pages. They don't decide what gets built next. We do. We own the roadmap. We prioritise by revenue impact. We tell them: "Here's what we're building this month and why." They approve and trust the system.

This changes everything:
- No more scope creep from ad-hoc client requests
- Every build is strategically sequenced for maximum impact
- The team moves faster without waiting for briefs
- Results compound because each build feeds into the next
- The client values the partnership more — they're buying expertise, not pixels

### Sell to Outcome, Not Deliverables

The retainer isn't a monthly page build — it's a share of recovered revenue. Frame it:

- Brand doing £800K/mo
- We get their site-wide CVR up 2%
- That's £16K/mo in recovered revenue minimum
- Retainer is £9K/mo — they're paying less than the value we create in month one

The maths sells itself. Stop talking about "what's included" and start talking about what the CVR gap is costing them. The retainer pays for itself at a 0.1% lift — everything above that is pure ROI.

This kills the common objections:
- No more "how many pages do I get?" conversations
- Value tied to revenue impact, not output volume
- Stickier clients — they see compounding results, not isolated projects
- Higher LTV — retainers run 6-12 months vs one-off 4-week builds
- No price negotiation — the number is backed by their own data

- Spending £20K+ on paid traffic (Meta, Google, TikTok)

### The Sweet Spot

Brands that currently see us as **too cheap**. They're spending £50-100K/mo on ads and their conversion partner should feel proportional to that investment. A £1.5K retainer signals "small vendor." An £8K retainer signals "serious conversion team."

We are the bridge between where they are now and where they need to be:
- **Dropshippers becoming real brands** — they've outgrown Fiverr designers but aren't ready for a Big 4 agency
- **Established brands without CRO** — they have the traffic, the product, the brand, but their post-click is 2 years old and nobody's testing
- **Brands burned by agencies** — they've cycled through 2-3 vendors and want a partner who actually compounds results instead of starting from scratch every time
