# Ecom Sales Funnel Mastery — Complete Funnel Map

## The Mental Model

A DTC ecommerce funnel is not a linear pipeline. It's a pressure system. Every layer either builds momentum toward purchase or bleeds it. Your job as a CRO practitioner is to find where the pressure drops and fix the seal.

The funnel has 10 layers. Each one has a **job**, and that job must be completed before the next layer can do its work. When a layer fails, it doesn't just reduce conversion at that step — it degrades everything downstream.

## The Funnel Architecture

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LAYER 1: TRAFFIC & AUDIENCE                                    │
│  Job: Deliver the right people with the right intent            │
│  Key metric: Traffic quality score (not just volume)            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 2: AD CREATIVE & MESSAGING                               │
│  Job: Stop the scroll, set expectations, generate qualified     │
│       click-through                                             │
│  Key metric: CTR + landing page engagement (not CTR alone)      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 3: LANDING PAGE / HOMEPAGE                               │
│  Job: Confirm the promise, orient the visitor, move them        │
│       toward a product                                          │
│  Key metric: Scroll depth + product page view rate              │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 4: PRODUCT DETAIL PAGE (PDP)                             │
│  Job: Build enough desire and trust to trigger add-to-cart      │
│  Key metric: PDP-to-ATC rate (benchmark: 8-12% DTC)            │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 5: CART                                                   │
│  Job: Maintain momentum, increase AOV, don't leak               │
│  Key metric: Cart-to-checkout rate (benchmark: 55-65%)          │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 6: CHECKOUT                                               │
│  Job: Remove every friction point, capture the transaction      │
│  Key metric: Checkout completion rate (benchmark: 28-35%)       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 7: POST-PURCHASE                                         │
│  Job: Maximise immediate revenue, begin the retention loop      │
│  Key metric: Post-purchase upsell take rate + review rate       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 8: RETENTION & LTV                                        │
│  Job: Turn one-time buyers into repeat customers                │
│  Key metric: 90-day repeat purchase rate (benchmark: 25-30%)    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 9: OFFER ARCHITECTURE (Cross-cutting)                    │
│  Job: Structure the commercial proposition to maximise          │
│       conversion, AOV, and margin simultaneously                │
│  Key metric: Revenue per session                                │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  LAYER 10: ANALYTICS & MEASUREMENT (Cross-cutting)              │
│  Job: Tell you what's actually happening vs what you think      │
│       is happening                                              │
│  Key metric: Decision accuracy (are you optimising the          │
│              right things?)                                     │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## The Metrics Stack

This is the core diagnostic framework. When conversion drops, walk this stack top-to-bottom to find where the bleed is.

| Stage | Metric | DTC Benchmark | What It Tells You |
|-------|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| Sessions | Total qualified sessions | Varies | Top of funnel volume |
| Bounce Rate | % single-page sessions | 35-55% (paid), 25-40% (organic) | Message-market match quality |
| Product Page Views | Sessions with PDP view | 40-60% of sessions | Navigation effectiveness |
| Add to Cart | ATC rate (from PDP) | 8-12% | PDP persuasion power |
| Cart View | Cart view rate | 65-80% of ATC | Cart accessibility |
| Checkout Initiated | Checkout rate (from ATC) | 55-65% | Cart-to-checkout friction |
| Checkout Completed | Purchase rate (from checkout) | 28-35% | Checkout friction |
| Overall CVR | Purchase / Sessions | 1.5-3.5% (paid cold), 3-6% (warm) | Funnel health |
| AOV | Average order value | Category-dependent | Offer architecture effectiveness |
| Revenue Per Session | Revenue / Sessions | $2-8 (DTC avg) | The single best health metric |

## The Interconnection Map

This is where most CRO practitioners fail. They optimise layers in isolation. But the funnel is a system — every layer affects every other layer.

### Critical Connections

**Traffic → Everything Downstream**
Cold traffic from a TikTok ad behaves completely differently from a warm email subscriber. A 3% CVR site with email traffic might be a 0.8% CVR site with cold paid social. The traffic source changes the benchmark for every downstream metric.

**Ad Creative → Landing Page (The Handoff)**
The #1 cause of high-CPC, low-conversion campaigns is a broken handoff. If the ad shows a lifestyle image of someone using the product, and the landing page opens with a product-on-white hero — you've broken the conversation. The visitor's brain has to re-orient. That costs you 20-40% of your traffic in the first 3 seconds.

**Landing Page → PDP (The Bridge)**
The landing page's job is NOT to sell. It's to get the right product in front of the right person. If your landing page has 200 products and no guidance, you're asking cold traffic to do the work your merchandising should do. Best-in-class DTC brands use curated landing pages that funnel traffic to 1-3 hero products.

**PDP → Cart (The Commitment Point)**
Add-to-cart is the first commitment. Everything on the PDP is building toward this moment. But the PDP also sets expectations for what happens next — if you show "free shipping" on the PDP and then reveal a £4.99 shipping fee in the cart, you've destroyed trust at the worst possible moment.

**Cart → Checkout (The Leak)**
The cart-to-checkout transition is where most DTC brands lose 35-45% of their would-be customers. The top reasons: unexpected costs revealed, required account creation, complicated navigation, and too many upsell distractions.

**Checkout → Post-Purchase (The Momentum)**
A smooth checkout creates a dopamine hit — the purchase decision is made. The thank-you page and post-purchase sequence must capitalise on this emotional high. This is when the customer is most receptive to upsells, reviews, and referrals.

**Post-Purchase → Retention (The Loop)**
The first 7 days after purchase determine whether you get a one-time buyer or a repeat customer. Speed of delivery, quality of unboxing, first email sequence — these set the emotional foundation for LTV.

## The Diagnostic Framework

When a client comes to you and says "our conversion rate dropped," here's how to diagnose:

### Step 1: Isolate the Layer
Pull the metrics stack. Find where the drop-off is steepest relative to benchmarks.

### Step 2: Check for External Factors
- Has traffic mix changed? (More cold traffic = lower CVR, and that's normal)
- Has seasonality shifted? (Post-holiday drop is not a CRO problem)
- Has a competitor launched? (Check auction dynamics on paid channels)
- Has something broken technically? (Page speed, mobile rendering, checkout errors)

### Step 3: Audit the Failing Layer
Use the layer-specific audit questions (see each module and AUDIT-CHECKLIST.md).

### Step 4: Check the Layer Above
Often the "failing" layer is fine — it's receiving lower-quality input from the layer above. A PDP with a 6% ATC rate might be excellent if the traffic coming to it is ice-cold from a broad awareness campaign.

### Step 5: Hypothesise, Prioritise, Test
Generate test hypotheses (see TEST-HYPOTHESES.md), score them with ICE, and run them in priority order.

## The Economics of Funnel Optimisation

Why CRO is the highest-ROI investment in DTC:

**Scenario:** A DTC brand doing £1M/year in revenue
- 500,000 sessions/year
- 2% CVR = 10,000 orders
- £100 AOV

**If you improve CVR from 2% to 2.5% (+25% relative):**
- 12,500 orders × £100 = £1.25M (+£250K revenue)
- No additional ad spend required
- That £250K flows almost entirely to gross profit

**If you improve AOV from £100 to £115 (+15%):**
- 10,000 orders × £115 = £1.15M (+£150K revenue)

**If you do both:**
- 12,500 × £115 = £1,437,500 (+£437.5K, or 43.75% revenue growth)

This is why CRO is the only growth lever that compounds. Every improvement you make benefits every future session.

## How to Use This Knowledge Base

1. **For client audits:** Start with the AUDIT-CHECKLIST.md. Walk through each layer's questions against the client's store. Score each layer 1-5.

2. **For test planning:** Go to the relevant layer module, review the test hypotheses, score them with ICE (Impact × Confidence × Ease, each 1-10), and prioritise.

3. **For training:** Have junior team members read one module per day, then audit a real store against that module's criteria.

4. **For client deliverables:** The "what good looks like" and "what bad looks like" sections give you specific, defensible language for client reports.
