# Glossary of DTC Ecommerce & CRO Terms

## Funnel Metrics

**Sessions** — Individual visits to the website. One person can generate multiple sessions. The top of the metrics stack.

**Unique Visitors** — Distinct individuals visiting the site (cookie-based, imperfect but directional). Lower than sessions because one visitor can have multiple sessions.

**Bounce Rate** — Percentage of sessions where the visitor views only one page and leaves. Benchmarks: 35-55% for paid traffic, 25-40% for organic. A high bounce rate usually signals broken message-market match, not a bad website.

**Pages Per Session** — Average number of pages viewed per session. Higher isn't always better — it can indicate confusion (can't find what they want) or engagement. Context matters.

**Product Page Views (PDP Views)** — Sessions where a visitor viewed at least one product detail page. Benchmark: 40-60% of sessions should reach a PDP. Below 40% = navigation or landing page problem.

**Add to Cart Rate (ATC Rate)** — Percentage of PDP views that result in an add-to-cart action. Benchmark: 8-12% for DTC. Calculated as ATC events / PDP views.

**Cart-to-Checkout Rate** — Percentage of visitors who added to cart and then initiated checkout. Benchmark: 55-65%. Calculated as checkout initiations / ATC events.

**Checkout Completion Rate** — Percentage of visitors who initiated checkout and completed purchase. Benchmark: 28-35%. The gap is where friction, trust, and cost anxiety live.

**Conversion Rate (CVR)** — Percentage of sessions that result in a purchase. Benchmarks: 1.5-3.5% for cold paid traffic, 3-6% for warm/returning traffic, 8-15% for email traffic. The single most-watched metric in ecommerce, but misleading without segmentation.

**Revenue Per Session (RPS)** — Total revenue divided by total sessions. The single best metric for overall funnel health because it captures both conversion rate AND average order value. Benchmark: $2-8 for typical DTC.

**Average Order Value (AOV)** — Total revenue divided by number of orders. Influenced by pricing, bundles, upsells, and free shipping thresholds. Category-dependent.

**Cart Abandonment Rate** — Percentage of visitors who add to cart but don't purchase. Global average: 69-72%. This number is misleading because it includes window shoppers and price comparers. Focus on recoverable abandonment instead.

**Checkout Abandonment Rate** — Percentage of visitors who initiate checkout but don't complete purchase. Benchmark: 65-72%. More actionable than cart abandonment because these visitors had higher intent.

## CRO (Conversion Rate Optimisation) Terms

**CRO** — Conversion Rate Optimisation. The practice of increasing the percentage of visitors who take a desired action (usually purchase). In practice, CRO encompasses conversion rate, AOV, and revenue per session.

**A/B Test (Split Test)** — An experiment where two versions of a page or element are shown to different visitor segments to determine which performs better. Requires statistical significance to be valid.

**Statistical Significance** — The probability that the observed difference between test variants is not due to random chance. Industry standard: 95% confidence (p < 0.05). Below this, the result is directional but not conclusive.

**Sample Size** — The number of visitors (or conversions) needed to reach statistical significance. Most DTC brands need ~1,000 conversions per variant per month to run valid tests. Below this, you're guessing.

**ICE Score** — Impact × Confidence × Ease. A prioritisation framework for test hypotheses. Each factor scored 1-10. Total score ranges from 3-30. Used to rank which tests to run first.

**Multivariate Test (MVT)** — Testing multiple variables simultaneously. Requires significantly more traffic than A/B tests. Rarely appropriate for DTC brands.

**Control** — The original, unchanged version in an A/B test. The baseline against which variations are measured.

**Variant (Treatment)** — The modified version in an A/B test. The challenger that you believe might outperform the control.

**Lift** — The percentage improvement of a variant over the control. Example: if control CVR is 2% and variant CVR is 2.4%, lift is 20%.

**Heatmap** — A visual overlay showing where visitors click, scroll, or hover on a page. Types: click maps, scroll maps, attention maps. Tools: Hotjar, Microsoft Clarity.

**Session Recording** — A video replay of a visitor's interaction with the site. Used for qualitative research to understand behaviour patterns. Watch 20-30 per segment minimum before drawing conclusions.

**Rage Click** — Repeated rapid clicking on an element, indicating frustration. Usually means something looks clickable but isn't, or a button isn't responding.

**Scroll Depth** — How far down a page visitors scroll, measured as a percentage. If only 30% of visitors see your CTA at 60% scroll depth, 70% of your traffic never sees it.

**Above the Fold** — Content visible without scrolling. On mobile, this is roughly the first 600-700px. On desktop, approximately 800-900px. Critical content (headline, CTA, social proof) should be above the fold.

**Below the Fold** — Content only visible after scrolling. Important for storytelling and objection handling, but the most critical conversion elements should be above the fold.

## Traffic & Audience Terms

**Traffic Source** — Where the visitor came from. Categories: paid social, paid search, organic, email, SMS, direct, referral, affiliate.

**Audience Temperature** — The visitor's familiarity and intent level. Cold (never heard of you), Warm (has engaged before), Hot (has purchased or is actively shopping).

**Message-Market Match** — The alignment between what the ad/channel promised and what the landing page delivers. A broken match = high bounce rate.

**UTM Parameters** — URL tags (source, medium, campaign, content, term) used to track where traffic comes from and attribute conversions. Example: `?utm_source=meta&utm_medium=paid&utm_campaign=spring_sale`

**CPM (Cost Per Mille)** — Cost per 1,000 impressions. A platform-level metric that reflects how expensive it is to reach an audience. Largely outside your control — driven by competition and audience quality.

**CTR (Click-Through Rate)** — Percentage of ad impressions that result in clicks. Benchmark: 1-3% for Meta ads. A direct measure of creative quality and message-market fit.

**CPC (Cost Per Click)** — Cost per individual click to your site. Calculated as CPM / (CTR × 10). The intersection of how much reach costs and how compelling your creative is.

**CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)** — Total ad spend divided by number of purchases. Also called CAC when including all marketing costs. The cost of acquiring one customer.

**CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — Total cost to acquire one customer, including all marketing spend, agency fees, and tooling. The denominator in the LTV:CAC ratio.

**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** — Revenue generated per pound/dollar of ad spend. Example: 4:1 ROAS means £4 revenue for every £1 spent. Breakeven ROAS depends on margin.

**MER (Marketing Efficiency Ratio)** — Total revenue / total marketing spend. A blended metric that captures all marketing channels, not just one. More honest than channel-specific ROAS.

**Retargeting** — Showing ads to visitors who have previously engaged (visited site, added to cart, etc.). Warmer audience, higher CVR, but limited scale.

**Prospecting** — Showing ads to people who haven't engaged with your brand before. Cold audience, lower CVR, but the engine of growth.

**Lookalike Audience** — An audience built by ad platforms based on similarity to your existing customers. Quality varies dramatically based on the seed audience and the platform's data.

## Product & Pricing Terms

**PDP (Product Detail Page)** — The individual product page. The most important page in DTC ecommerce — this is where the purchase decision is made.

**SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)** — A unique identifier for each product variant. Example: "Black Wallet — Regular" is a different SKU from "Black Wallet — Large."

**Variant** — A specific version of a product (size, color, material). How variants are displayed (swatches vs dropdowns) significantly affects conversion.

**AOV (Average Order Value)** — Total revenue divided by total orders. Increase AOV through bundles, upsells, free shipping thresholds, and subscription offers.

**COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)** — The direct cost of producing the product. Determines gross margin. Critical for understanding whether discounts are sustainable.

**Gross Margin** — (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue. The percentage of revenue remaining after product costs. Determines how much you can spend on acquisition and still be profitable.

**Contribution Margin** — Revenue minus all variable costs (COGS, shipping, payment processing, packaging). The true per-order profitability metric.

**Anchoring** — A pricing psychology technique where a higher reference price (e.g., "Was £79.99") is shown to make the current price feel like a better deal.

**BNPL (Buy Now, Pay Later)** — Payment options like Klarna, Afterpay, Clearpay that allow customers to split payments. Increases conversion 10-20% for orders over £50 by reducing payment pain.

**Price Elasticity** — How sensitive demand is to price changes. Low elasticity (luxury, unique products) means you can raise prices without losing volume. High elasticity (commoditised products) means small price changes significantly affect demand.

## UX & Design Terms

**CTA (Call to Action)** — A button or link that prompts the visitor to take a specific action. Primary CTA: the main action you want (e.g., "Add to Cart"). Secondary CTA: an alternative action (e.g., "Learn More").

**Hero Section** — The first visible section of a page, typically including headline, subheadline, imagery, and primary CTA. Must pass the 5-second test.

**5-Second Test** — Show someone a page for 5 seconds, then ask: What does this company sell? What makes them different? What should I do next? If they can't answer, the hero section is failing.

**Sticky CTA** — A CTA button that remains fixed on screen as the user scrolls (typically at the bottom on mobile). Ensures the action button is always accessible.

**Social Proof** — Evidence that other people have purchased and are satisfied. Includes reviews, ratings, testimonials, UGC, press mentions, and real-time activity indicators.

**UGC (User-Generated Content)** — Photos, videos, and content created by actual customers. More trusted than brand-created content for cold traffic. Used in ads, PDPs, and social media.

**Trust Signals** — Elements that reduce anxiety and build confidence: security badges, payment logos, guarantees, return policies, press mentions, certifications.

**Risk Reversal** — Removing the perceived risk of purchase. "30-day money-back guarantee, no questions asked" is classic risk reversal. The more specific the guarantee, the more effective it is.

**Cognitive Load** — The mental effort required to process information. High cognitive load = more decisions, more complexity, more abandonment. Good UX reduces cognitive load.

**Choice Paralysis (Paradox of Choice)** — Too many options cause indecision and abandonment. If a PDP has 47 color variants in a grid, the visitor is more likely to leave than choose.

**Thumb Zone** — The area of a mobile screen easily reachable with one thumb. CTAs and interactive elements should be in the natural thumb zone (lower portion of screen for right-handed users, which is the majority).

**Tap Target** — A clickable/tappable element. Minimum size: 44x44px. Elements smaller than this cause frustration and accidental taps on mobile.

## Email & Retention Terms

**LTV (Lifetime Value)** — The total revenue (or profit) a customer generates over their entire relationship with your brand. LTV should be calculated by cohort, not as a blended average.

**LTV:CAC Ratio** — Lifetime Value divided by Customer Acquisition Cost. Healthy DTC benchmark: 3:1 or higher. Below 2:1, acquisition is unsustainable.

**Repeat Purchase Rate** — Percentage of customers who purchase more than once. DTC benchmark: 25-30% within 90 days for consumables.

**RFM Model** — Recency (how recently they purchased), Frequency (how often), Monetary (how much they've spent). Used to segment customers for targeted communication.

**Flow (Email Flow / Automation)** — An automated email sequence triggered by a specific action. Examples: welcome flow, abandoned cart flow, post-purchase flow, win-back flow.

**Campaign (Email Campaign)** — A one-time email sent to a segment. Different from flows, which are automated and triggered.

**Open Rate** — Percentage of recipients who opened an email. Benchmark: 30-50% for flows, 15-25% for campaigns. Inflated since iOS 15 (Mail Privacy Protection opens emails automatically).

**Click Rate** — Percentage of recipients who clicked a link. More reliable than open rate post-iOS 15. Benchmark: 2-5% for campaigns, 3-8% for flows.

**Revenue Per Recipient (RPR)** — Revenue generated per email recipient. The best email performance metric because it captures both engagement and conversion.

**Churn Rate** — Percentage of subscription customers who cancel in a given period. Monthly churn of 5-8% is common for DTC subscriptions; below 5% is excellent.

**Win-Back** — A flow or campaign targeting customers who haven't purchased in a defined period (typically 60-90 days for DTC). Goal: reactivate before they become fully lapsed.

**Sunset Flow** — The process of removing unengaged email subscribers from your list. Important for deliverability — sending to unengaged contacts hurts your sender reputation.

## Offer & Promotion Terms

**Offer** — The complete proposition presented to the customer. Includes product, price, discount, bundle, guarantee, and shipping terms. The same product with different offers converts differently.

**Bundle** — Two or more products sold together, typically at a discount vs buying individually. Productised bundles (named, curated) outperform dynamic bundles (pick your own) for simplicity.

**Order Bump** — A small add-on offered during checkout, typically as a checkbox. "Add extended warranty — £4.99." The highest-ROI element in checkout with 10-25% take rates.

**Upsell** — Offering a higher-value alternative. "Upgrade to the 3-pack and save 20%." Different from cross-sell (which is a complementary product).

**Cross-sell** — Offering a complementary product. "Customers also bought this case." Works best when the suggestion is genuinely relevant.

**Downsell** — Offering a lower-value alternative when the visitor is about to abandon. "Not ready for the full kit? Start with the mini." Used in exit-intent and follow-up emails.

**Free Shipping Threshold** — The minimum order value for free shipping. Optimal: 20-30% above current AOV. Example: AOV is £50, set threshold at £65.

**Loss Leader** — A product sold at or below cost to attract customers, with the expectation of profit from subsequent purchases. Common strategy for subscription brands.

## Technical Terms

**Page Speed** — How fast a page loads. Measured by Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS). Every 100ms of additional load time costs approximately 1% of conversion.

**LCP (Largest Contentful Paint)** — Time until the largest visible element loads. Target: under 2.5 seconds. The primary page speed metric for perceived performance.

**FID (First Input Delay)** — Time until the page responds to the first user interaction. Target: under 100ms. Replaced by INP (Interaction to Next Paint) in Core Web Vitals.

**CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift)** — How much page content shifts during loading. Target: under 0.1. Layout shift is disorienting and causes accidental clicks. Common culprit: images without explicit dimensions.

**Server-Side Tracking** — Sending conversion data from your server to ad platforms (instead of relying on browser-based pixels). More reliable post-iOS 14.5 privacy changes.

**Pixel** — A small piece of code on your website that tracks visitor behavior and sends data to ad platforms (Meta Pixel, TikTok Pixel, etc.). Accuracy degraded by privacy changes.

**CAPI (Conversions API)** — Server-to-server data sharing with ad platforms. The replacement for browser-based pixels. Essential for accurate conversion tracking and ad optimisation.

**GA4 (Google Analytics 4)** — Google's current analytics platform. Event-based (vs session-based like Universal Analytics). Required for modern ecommerce tracking.

**Shopify Checkout Extensibility** — Shopify Plus feature that allows customisation of the checkout experience. Required for order bumps, custom trust signals, and checkout layout changes on Shopify.

**Shop Pay** — Shopify's accelerated checkout. Stores payment info across all Shopify stores. Converts 1.7x better than standard checkout according to Shopify's data.
