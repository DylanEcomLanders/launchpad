# FAQ — Client & Internal

## Client-Facing FAQs

### "How many pages do I get per month?"

That's not how this works — and that's the whole point. A vendor gives you X pages per month. A conversion partner gives you a system that compounds revenue. Some months it's 3 landing pages. Some months it's a full PDP overhaul plus checkout optimisation plus a post-purchase upsell flow. We prioritise by revenue impact, not page count. You're not buying pages — you're buying conversion improvement.

### "Why is it £8K+? I can get landing pages for £500."

You can. And those £500 pages will sit there doing nothing because they weren't part of a strategy, they weren't tested, and nobody's measuring whether they work. The difference between a page build and a conversion partnership is the system: audit → roadmap → build → test → measure → compound. The page is 20% of the value. The other 80% is knowing what to build, why to build it, and what to do when the data comes back.

The maths: if your CVR gap is costing you £100K/mo in lost revenue, an £8K partnership that recovers even 10% of that is a 125% ROI. The £500 page with no strategy behind it recovers nothing.

### "How long until I see results?"

Month 1 is audit and roadmap — you'll see exactly where the leaks are and what we're fixing first. Quick wins go live in Week 1. First major builds go live Month 2. Most clients see measurable CVR improvement within the first 90 days. The compounding effect kicks in from Month 3 onwards — each test informs the next build, each build generates data for the next test.

### "What if it doesn't work?"

Every build is a hypothesis with a predicted outcome. If something doesn't hit, we learn from it and iterate. That's the whole point of a partnership vs a one-off build — you get the learning loop, not just a page. If after 90 days the numbers genuinely haven't moved, we'll have an honest conversation. But in our experience, the maths works when the traffic is there.

### "Can I cancel anytime?"

Yes. No lock-in. We keep clients because the maths works, not because of a contract. That said — CRO compounds. Leaving after 2 months is like pulling out of the gym after 2 weeks. The results are just starting to show. We recommend a minimum 90-day commitment to see the system work.

### "Do you work with our existing dev team?"

Yes. We design and build everything. If you want your dev team to implement, we hand over clean Figma files with section-by-section specs and development notes. Most clients prefer we handle everything end-to-end — that's how we move fastest and maintain quality.

### "Do you handle the ad creative too?"

Our focus is post-click — what happens after the ad. But we do align every landing page with your ad creative so the scent trail is consistent. If your ad promises X, the page delivers X. We don't run ads — but we make your ads work harder by converting more of the traffic they send.

### "What platforms do you work with?"

Shopify and Shopify Plus primarily. 90% of our clients are on Shopify. We've done work on custom builds but the system is optimised for Shopify's ecosystem.

### "How is this different from a CRO agency?"

Most CRO agencies audit and recommend. They send you a report with 47 suggestions and leave you to implement them. We audit, recommend, AND execute. Strategy and builds from the same team. No handoff gaps, no "the agency said X but the designers did Y." We also go beyond traditional CRO — we handle AOV optimisation, post-purchase flows, and offer strategy, not just A/B testing button colours.

### "We already tested landing pages and they didn't work."

A page that didn't convert isn't a failed test — it's an unfinished experiment. Did you test the offer? The audience match? The page structure? The traffic source? One page is a guess. A conversion programme is a process of elimination. The brands getting 3-4% CVR didn't get there by building one page.

### "Why not just hire someone in-house?"

A senior CRO hire costs £60-80K/year plus tools, training, and 6 months to ramp up. And you get one person. Our partnership gives you a full team — strategist, designer, developer, QA — with 5,000+ builds behind us. For a fraction of one hire. And we're productive from Week 1, not Month 6.

### "We want to start with a single project first."

We do offer project work. But here's the truth: a single project gives you a page. A partnership gives you a system. The brands that see 2x CVR improvement aren't doing one-off builds — they're running a programme where each month's work builds on the last. If budget is the concern, look at the revenue gap numbers — the partnership usually pays for itself in month one.

### "Our brand is different / our audience is unique."

Every brand thinks they're unique. And they are — in their product, their audience, their story. But the conversion principles are universal. The same psychological frameworks that drive conversions for supplements work for fashion, pet products, and food brands. We adapt the strategy to your vertical, but the system is proven across 50+ DTC brands.

## Internal FAQs

### "Client wants to brief specific pages instead of following the roadmap."

Redirect firmly: "We prioritise by revenue impact. If there's a specific page you want built, we can look at it — but let us show you why [our priority] should come first based on the data." If they insist, do it but flag it in your notes. If this becomes a pattern, have a partnership health conversation: "The value of this model is that we own the strategy. If you want to brief individual pages, that's a vendor relationship at a different price point."

### "Client wants to reduce scope or pause."

Understand why. If it's budget pressure, offer to pause for a month rather than reduce scope. Half-scope = half-results = churn in 2 months anyway. Reframe to ROI: "Reducing scope now means the roadmap takes twice as long to deliver results. The partnership is designed to deliver X ROI — at half-scope, the maths changes." If they're genuinely not getting results after 90 days, be honest about it.

### "Client isn't giving us analytics access."

Red flag. Push back firmly: "We need GA4 and Shopify access to measure impact and prioritise correctly. Without data, we're guessing — and you're paying us not to guess." If they refuse after the second ask, consider whether this is the right client. No data = no proof of value = they'll churn.

### "Client wants changes that go against our recommendation."

Present the data. "We recommend X because [evidence]. We can do Y if you prefer, but here's the risk." Document it. If they insist, do it their way but track the outcome separately. Never say "I told you so" — say "the data showed."

### "Client asking for work outside post-click (email, ads, social)."

Stay in lane. "That's outside our core — we focus on the conversion layer because that's where we deliver the most impact. We'd recommend [partner/agency] for [email/ads/social]." Don't scope-creep into areas where you can't guarantee results. Exception: if they want post-purchase email flow *design* (not implementation), that's within our scope.

### "How do I handle a client who's churning?"

Get on a call. Ask directly: "What would need to change for this to work?" Often it's a communication gap, not a results gap. Pull up the data — every test run, every CVR movement, every page shipped, every revenue number. If the numbers are there, it's a perception problem (fix with better reporting). If the numbers genuinely aren't there after 90 days, part ways professionally and learn from it.

### "New prospect says we're too expensive."

If the maths doesn't work for their brand (traffic too low, revenue too low, CVR already good), they're not the right client. Walk away — don't discount. "The partnership is designed for brands where the revenue gap justifies the investment. Based on your numbers, it might not make sense right now. When you're doing [X traffic/revenue], let's revisit."

If the maths DOES work and they still say it's too expensive, they're comparing you to vendors. Reframe: "You're not comparing like-for-like. A £1.5K vendor gives you pages. This partnership gives you a full conversion system with a team behind it. The ROI at your traffic level is [X]. The question isn't whether you can afford it — it's whether you can afford the £[gap] you're losing every month without it."
