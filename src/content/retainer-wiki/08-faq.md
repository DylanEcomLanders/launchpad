# FAQ

## Client-Facing FAQs

### "How many pages do I get per month?"
It depends on the roadmap. Some months it's 3 landing pages. Some months it's a full PDP overhaul plus checkout optimisation. We prioritise by revenue impact, not page count. You're not buying pages — you're buying conversion improvement.

### "How long until I see results?"
Month 1 is audit and roadmap — you'll see exactly where the leaks are and what we're fixing first. First builds go live Month 2. Most clients see measurable CVR improvement within the first 90 days. The compounding effect kicks in from Month 3 onwards.

### "What if it doesn't work?"
Every test is a hypothesis with a predicted outcome. If a test doesn't hit, we learn something and iterate. That's the whole point of a retainer vs a one-off build — you get the learning loop, not just a page. If after 90 days the numbers haven't moved, we'll have an honest conversation.

### "Can I cancel anytime?"
Yes. No lock-in. We keep clients because the maths works, not because of a contract. That said — CRO compounds. Leaving after 2 months is like pulling out of the gym after 2 weeks. The results are just starting.

### "Do you work with our existing dev team?"
Yes. We design and build the pages. If you want your dev team to implement, we hand over clean Figma files with section-by-section specs. Most clients prefer we handle everything end-to-end.

### "Do you handle the ad creative too?"
Our focus is post-click — what happens after the ad. But we do align landing pages with your ad angles so the scent trail is consistent. If your ads promise X, the page delivers X. We don't run ads.

### "What platforms do you work with?"
Shopify and Shopify Plus primarily. We've done work on custom builds but 90% of our clients are on Shopify.

### "How is this different from hiring a CRO agency?"
Most CRO agencies audit and recommend. We audit, recommend, AND execute. You get the strategy and the builds from the same team. No handoff gaps, no "the agency said X but the designers did Y."

### "We already tested landing pages and they didn't work."
A page that didn't convert isn't a failed test — it's an unfinished experiment. Did you test the offer? The audience match? The page structure? One page is a guess. A programme is a process of elimination.

### "Why not just hire someone in-house?"
A senior CRO hire costs £60-80K/year plus tools, training, and management overhead. Our retainer gives you a full team — strategist, designer, developer, QA — for a fraction of that. And we've done this 5,000+ times.

## Internal FAQs

### "Client wants to reduce scope — what do I do?"
Understand why. If it's budget pressure, offer to pause for a month rather than reduce scope. Half-scope = half-results = churn in 2 months anyway. If they want fewer pages, reframe: "It's not about page count — which conversion leaks do you want us to stop fixing?"

### "Client isn't giving us access to analytics."
Red flag. Push back firmly: "We need GA4 and Shopify access to measure impact and prioritise correctly. Without data, we're guessing — and you're paying us not to guess." If they still refuse, consider whether this is the right client.

### "Client wants changes that go against our recommendation."
Present the data. "We recommend X because [evidence]. We can do Y if you prefer, but here's the risk." Document it. If they insist, do it their way but flag it in the monthly report. Never say "I told you so" — say "the data showed."

### "Client asking for work outside post-click (email, ads, social)."
Stay in lane. "That's outside our core — we focus on post-click because that's where we deliver the most impact. Happy to recommend someone for [email/ads/social]." Don't scope-creep into areas where you can't guarantee results.

### "How do I handle a client who's churning?"
Get on a call. Ask directly: "What would need to change for this to be worth it?" Often it's a communication gap, not a results gap. Show the data — every test run, every CVR movement, every page shipped. If the numbers genuinely aren't there after 90 days, part ways professionally.
