# Onboarding & System Setup

## The First 7 Days

Once the partnership starts, onboarding moves fast. The client needs to feel momentum immediately — they just committed to £8K+/mo and the clock is ticking. No slow ramp-ups.

### Day 1-2: Access & Data Collection

**Get access to everything immediately:**
- [ ] GA4 viewer access (or GA data export if they're protective)
- [ ] Shopify analytics access
- [ ] Heatmap tool access (Hotjar, MS Clarity, Lucky Orange)
- [ ] Ad accounts (Meta, Google — view only for creative analysis)
- [ ] Brand guidelines (logo, fonts, colours, tone of voice)
- [ ] Product photography / assets folder (Google Drive or similar)
- [ ] Top 5 products by revenue
- [ ] Current ad creatives (or Meta Ad Library review)
- [ ] Any existing research (customer surveys, reviews, NPS, post-purchase surveys)
- [ ] Key stakeholder identified (ONE person for approvals — not a committee)

**Set up comms:**
- [ ] Dedicated Slack channel created (us + their key person)
- [ ] Weekly update rhythm agreed (async update or 15-min call)
- [ ] Monthly strategy call booked (30-60 mins)

### Day 3-5: The Deep Conversion Audit

This is the full audit — not the pre-sales version. Now we have backend data.

1. **Full Conversion Matrix with real data** — Score every funnel layer using actual drop-off rates, device-level CVR splits, and page-level performance. Validate or adjust the pre-sales scores.
2. **Heatmap analysis** — Where are users clicking? Where do they stop scrolling? What gets ignored? Pull data from top 5 highest-traffic pages.
3. **Funnel drop-off analysis** — Map the exact path: landing page → PDP → cart → checkout → purchase. Where's the biggest leak?
4. **Competitor benchmark** — Screenshot top 3 competitors' post-click experiences. What are they doing that our client isn't?
5. **AOV opportunity mapping** — Bundles, upsells, cross-sells, post-purchase offers. Where is money being left on the table beyond CVR?
6. **Copy and offer audit** — Is the value proposition clear? Is the offer compelling? Does the messaging match the ad creative?

### Day 5-7: The Roadmap Build

This is the deliverable that defines the entire partnership.

**The 60-90 Day Visual Roadmap:**

Built in Miro (or equivalent). Not a spreadsheet — a visual, strategic document the client can see and understand at a glance.

The roadmap includes:
- **Month 1 priorities** — The 2-3 highest-impact conversion assets to build first
- **Month 2 priorities** — Second wave builds, informed by Month 1 data
- **Month 3 priorities** — Testing iterations, optimisation, expansion
- **Testing roadmap** — If/then hypothesis chains for each build
- **AOV roadmap** — Upsell, cross-sell, and post-purchase opportunities sequenced
- **Quick wins** — Things we can fix in Week 1 without a full build (broken links, missing trust signals, checkout friction)

**Every item on the roadmap is:**
- Prioritised by revenue impact (ICE scored)
- Tied to a specific conversion metric (CVR, ATC rate, AOV)
- Connected to an if/then hypothesis
- Sequenced so each build feeds data into the next

> **Critical:** The client does NOT brief this roadmap. We own it. We present it at kickoff as "here's what we're doing and why." They approve the direction, not the individual deliverables.

## Client Kickoff Meeting

**When:** Day 7-10 (after audit and roadmap are complete)

**Duration:** 45-60 minutes

**Agenda:**
1. **Audit findings walkthrough** — "Here's what we found and here's what it's costing you" (15 mins)
2. **Visual roadmap presentation** — Walk through the 90-day plan on Miro (15 mins)
3. **Month 1 deliverables confirmed** — Specific pages/builds starting this week (5 mins)
4. **Communication rhythm agreed** — Weekly async updates, monthly calls (5 mins)
5. **Quick wins identified** — Things going live in the next 48 hours (5 mins)
6. **Questions and alignment** (10 mins)

**The client should leave this call thinking:**
- "These people know our business better than our in-house team"
- "The plan is clear and I can see where the ROI comes from"
- "I don't need to manage this — they've got it"

## Onboarding Red Flags

Watch for these early — they predict churn:

| Red Flag | What It Means | How to Handle |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Multiple approval stakeholders | Slow decisions, design-by-committee | Push for one decision maker. "Speed matters — one person approves, everyone else gives input." |
| No analytics access | They don't trust you yet, or they don't have analytics set up properly | Firm but fair: "We need data to prioritise correctly. Without it, we're guessing — and you're paying us not to guess." |
| Vague goals | "Just make it better" = no way to prove value | Frame specific targets: "Let's aim for a 1% CVR lift in 90 days. Here's what that's worth in revenue." |
| Price sensitivity in Month 1 | "Can we reduce scope this month?" = they're already questioning the investment | Reframe to ROI: "The first 90 days is the foundation. Reducing scope now is like building half a bridge." |
| No ad spend | "We're pausing ads" = no traffic = no testing = no results | Be honest: "Our system needs traffic to work. If ads are paused, we should pause too and restart when traffic is back." |
| Wants to control the roadmap | "Can you build this page instead?" = vendor mentality, not partner mentality | Redirect: "We prioritise by revenue impact. If you want this page, we can add it — but here's why [our priority] should come first." |

## The Handover Document

After kickoff, the team receives an internal handover:

1. **Client brief** — Brand overview, products, audience, tone
2. **Full Conversion Matrix scores** — With notes on each layer
3. **90-day roadmap** — Miro link with priorities sequenced
4. **Month 1 design briefs** — Section-by-section breakdowns for the first builds
5. **Asset library** — Logos, fonts, photography, brand guidelines
6. **Key contacts** — Who approves, who gives feedback, response time expectations
7. **Red flags and notes** — Any onboarding concerns or special handling needed
