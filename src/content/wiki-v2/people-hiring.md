Hiring is one of the highest-leverage things we do. We try to do it deliberately, on a contract-first basis, with both founders involved at every stage.

## Where we look

- **Referral** — the strongest source. People who know how we work tend to know the right kind of person to recommend.
- **LinkedIn** — the main outbound source. Skill-based: we use LinkedIn's screening to filter by hard requirements before any conversation starts.

## The process

1. **Screen** — LinkedIn's screening filters by skill and basic requirements.
2. **Vibe-check call** — Ajay or Dylan organises a first-round conversation. Half competency, half "would we enjoy working with this person."
3. **Role conversation** — if the first call goes well, a deeper discussion about the role, expectations, and whether the fit holds up.
4. **Offer** — typically a contract with a probationary period, discussed openly with the candidate. Both Dylan and Ajay are on hand through interviewing and the decision.

## What "yes" looks like

A "yes" is the combination of four things, in roughly this order:

| Criterion | What we're looking for |
| --- | --- |
| Vibe | Someone we'd actively want to work with |
| Competency | Demonstrably able to do the role |
| English fluency | Clear written and spoken English |
| Skill depth | More than capable in the discipline, ideally with strong ecom or Shopify experience |

Any one of those missing is a no.

## Contract-first

Almost every hire starts as a contract with a probationary window. It gives both sides a clean way to test the fit before anything more permanent. The exact terms are discussed openly with the candidate up front.
