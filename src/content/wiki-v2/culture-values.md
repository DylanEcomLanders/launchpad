These are the behaviours we expect from each other and the ones we want clients to recognise in us. None of them are abstract. Read them as instructions, not aspirations.

## We guide, we don't bow

Clients pay us because we know more about conversion than they do. Saying yes to every request makes us a vendor, not a partner. When a request will hurt the work or the result, we push back. Politely, with reasoning, but firmly. We're here to produce the best work we can, and sometimes that means putting our foot down.

## We deliver on time, and we communicate when we can't

Hitting deadlines is the default. When something is at risk, the client hears about it 48 hours ahead, not when the day arrives. A healthy client relationship is the most valuable asset we have, and it's built almost entirely on trust around timing and communication.

## We chase results, not deliverables

The pages we ship are part of the process. They are not the point. The point is revenue, CVR, AOV: the numbers that move because of the work. Every brief, every test, every handoff should serve that outcome. If we shipped a beautiful page that didn't move the metric, we didn't actually deliver.

## We compete up, not down

We want to be the funnel agency. The one ecommerce founders name when asked who to call. That means competing with agencies ten times our size on quality, ambition, and outcomes, not with the studios in our own tank. We hold ourselves to that standard internally before anyone else holds us to it externally.

## We're helpful, friendly, respectful, responsive

Every conversation with us, top to bottom, should leave a client feeling held and valued. Quick replies, warm tone, no jargon games, no chasing. Respect runs internally too. We don't talk about clients badly, we don't slag off the team they had before us, and we don't talk down to each other.

> **What we are not**
>
> - We don't say yes to keep the peace when no is the better answer
> - We don't pad timelines to look like heroes when we hit them
> - We don't go silent when things slip, even when the news is awkward
> - We don't ship deliverables just to fill a sprint
> - We don't blame previous teams, other agencies, or each other when something goes wrong
