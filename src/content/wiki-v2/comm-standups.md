We don't do daily standups. The default mode of work is async, so a single weekly sync is enough as long as the rest of the work moves between meetings.

## Cadence

**Once a week per pod.** Twenty minutes, on GMeet or in a Slack Huddle. Same day each week so it's a fixture, not a calendar negotiation.

## Structure

The standup looks ahead, not back. The week is the unit.

- **Early in the week** — what's getting shipped this week, who owns what, anything in the way.
- **End of the week** — a quick review of what landed, then lock in next week's plan.

Either pattern works; the point is the pod leaves aligned on the week.

## Who runs it

The **PM runs the standup** by default. The Strategist joins when there's anything strategic to chew on for the pod's clients. Whoever runs it leaves a written summary in the pod's internal Slack channel afterwards so anyone who missed it can catch up async.

## What it isn't

> **Standup is for alignment, not work.**

Not a status report. Not a place to debug code or critique a layout. Anything that needs more than a sentence belongs in Slack or its own call.
