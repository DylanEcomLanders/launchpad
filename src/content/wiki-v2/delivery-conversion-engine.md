The Conversion Engine is the methodology behind the offer. Not CRO in the small-tweaks sense. A holistic, repeatable system for finding the biggest levers in a brand's funnel and pulling them.

## What it is, what it isn't

**Traditional CRO** is small tweaks for marginal differences. Button colours, headline variants, copy A/B tests on a single page in isolation. Best practice still plays its part, but it's the floor, not the work.

**The Conversion Engine** maps the whole funnel, finds the leaks, and pulls bigger levers. Larger lifts come from bigger decisions: positioning, hero offer, sequencing, what's on a page versus what's missing. Marginal tweaks come along for the ride but they're not the goal.

> **CRO tweaks pages. The Conversion Engine rebuilds funnels.**

## The 90-day arc

Each Conversion Engine engagement runs 90 days, structured as three monthly cycles.

**Month 1**

- **Week 1** — strategy + quick wins. The audit is synthesised into a clear plan. Anything we can ship immediately to start moving the needle goes out this week.
- **Weeks 2-3** — build. The pod ships the strategy.
- **Week 4** — test and hypothesise. Whatever shipped gets measured; new hypotheses feed into next month's plan.

**Months 2 and 3**

Same shape, less weight on strategy. Less of a full strategy week, more **mapping the next month's tests** and designing new conversion assets for the funnel.

The cycle repeats so each month builds on the data from the last.

## Why the hero offer is the central lever

The Conversion Engine works as a **partnership**, not a project. Growth comes from compounding decisions across months, not from any single page. That's why the offer is structured around 90 days. Short enough to commit to, long enough to show up.

## How we decide what to test

**Data-driven, but not blindly.** The data tells us where the leak is; gut and craft tell us what to put in its place. We're comfortable shipping a clear winner without an A/B if the evidence is unambiguous. We're also comfortable thinking outside the box when the data is silent.

We never test for the sake of testing.

## Reporting

The Strategist delivers a **weekly update** to the client showing:

- What shipped
- What the data is saying
- What's planned next

Continuous, written, in the external Slack channel. No surprise readouts at the end of the engagement.
