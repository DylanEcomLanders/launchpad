The values say what we believe. This page is what that looks like during a normal week.

## The shape of the day

The team is a mix: some are retained freelancers in lead roles, others are deliverables-based freelancers brought in by the work that's on. The default shape is UK 9 to 5, but we work around client timezones and around what people do best. Some designers ship more between 6pm and midnight than most do at 11am, and that's fine as long as the work moves.

Inside the 9 to 5, the default is async. We're trying to ship, not sit on calls. Slack carries most of the day. We pull people onto calls when async genuinely can't carry the conversation, not as a first instinct.

## How decisions get made

Pods are built to decide as a unit. Day-to-day calls happen inside the pod and don't need anyone outside it. Pod leads own the call in their lane: strategist on strategy, primary designer on a design element that isn't tied to strategy, primary dev on implementation choices. The lane defines who decides.

Above the pods sit the discipline Heads: Head Designer, Head Developer, Head PM, Head Strategist, plus the founders covering Operations and Sales. Heads don't sit inside pods. They oversee their function across every pod, set the bar for what "good" looks like in their discipline, and take the call when something sits above a pod lead's authority or crosses between pods.

When a pod can't resolve something internally, it goes to the relevant Head first. The PM leverages the Heads to keep work moving and standards consistent. Founders only get pulled in for bigger calls: direction of the engagement, retainer renewals, scope, anything commercial. We're still in and amongst the work, so this is a feature, not a bottleneck.

The history of how a call was made lives in Slack channels. Anything strategic about a client lives as a core note in the client area inside Launchpad so it doesn't disappear into a thread.

## What ownership looks like

The pod is assigned to the client, which makes the pod accountable for delivery. Inside the pod, ownership shifts by phase: the designer owns the design, the dev owns the build, the strategist owns the thinking that runs underneath both.

Across the pods, the Heads own the discipline. They don't ship client work themselves (that's the pod's job), but they set the standard for what good looks like in design, dev, strategy, and project management, and they review the output to hold it.

> **A pod ships the work. The Head holds the bar.**

When work hands off, the receiving role owns it in their phase. A dev finding a problem in the design pulls the pod designer in to fix it. We don't ship a broken thing because "it was like that in design."

Primaries own core net-new work, the page that didn't exist this morning. Secondaries own revisions, edits, and the quick fixes that keep things moving. Primary designer on a core page, primary dev on the core build. Secondaries free the primaries to keep new work flowing.

## How we disagree

Disagreement inside a pod is normal and useful. The way we hold it matters more than the disagreement itself. Question to understand, not to challenge. The point is to see what the other person sees, not to win the call.

When something can't be resolved inside the pod, the relevant Head settles it. If the Head can't, or it cuts across disciplines, the PM steps in. If it's still unresolved or commercially loaded, it goes to the founders. Deadline first: if a disagreement is halting progress, escalate fast. We'd rather make a slightly imperfect call on time than a perfect call late.

Pushback to the Heads and the founders is expected and welcomed. The team telling us we're wrong is one of the ways we get better.
