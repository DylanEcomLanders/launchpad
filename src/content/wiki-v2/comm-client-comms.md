How we talk to clients, who handles what, and where the lines are.

## Channel

All client communication runs through Slack, specifically the `#external-[client]` channel for that client. Email and other tools are exceptions, not defaults.

## Single point of contact

The **PM is the client's primary contact** today. When the Customer Success Manager (CSM) role is in place, the front-line shifts to a PM + CSM partnership: the PM handles project-level coordination, the CSM owns relationship management and is often the calmer voice on harder conversations.

The client should always know who their POC is. They shouldn't have to figure out who to message.

## Cadence

Clients hear from us continuously through the external Slack channel during the week. The Strategist sends a **weekly written update** with what shipped, what's planned, and what the data is showing.

Specific touchpoints (kickoff calls, mid-engagement reviews, end-of-engagement readouts) happen as the engagement demands.

## Saying no

When a client asks for something we don't think they should do, the conversation is handled by the **CSM and PM together**. The PM brings the technical reasoning from the pod; the CSM translates it into language that lands, without jargon and without making the client feel talked down to.

The point is never to win the argument. It's to make sure the client understands why we're pushing back and that they're better off for it.
