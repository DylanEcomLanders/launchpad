We run three layers: founders, four cross-pod leads, and a delivery pod assigned to each client. The team is intentionally small. Everyone has one clear job and the lines between roles are explicit so things don't fall between them.

> **Founders set direction. Heads hold the bar. Pods deliver the work.**

## Founders

Dylan and Ajay run lean across acquisition, content, and high-level direction. Both still hands-on in client work as needed, primarily in a consulting capacity. Either founder takes any escalation that's large, financial, or commercially loaded. Pushback and complaints land here too. Hiring decisions involve both.

| Founder | Owns |
| --- | --- |
| Dylan | Finance, operations, internal processes, day-to-day running |
| Ajay | Acquisition, sales calls, content, new business |

## Cross-pod leads

Four people sit above the pods and own a discipline across the whole team. They set the standard, review the output, and take the calls that sit above a pod's authority. Heads also have line-management input for their discipline.

There's one Strategist and one PM. Both serve every pod from above *and* deliver client work in their lane. Head Designer and Head Developer don't ship client work themselves. Their job is the standard, the review, and the call.

| Role | Owns | Delivers client work |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Head Strategist | All client strategy across every pod | Yes |
| Head PM | Project management across every pod | Yes |
| Head Designer | The design standard across pods, holds the quality bar | No |
| Head Developer | The development standard across pods, holds the quality bar | No |

## Pod roles

Each pod is a four-person delivery cell assigned to its clients. The Strategist and the PM are not "in" the pod. They serve every pod from above. Inside the pod itself, design and dev each have a primary and a secondary.

| Role | Owns |
| --- | --- |
| Primary Designer | Core net-new design work, the page that didn't exist this morning |
| Secondary Designer | Revisions, edits, and the quick fixes that keep things moving |
| Primary Dev | Core build work, new pages, and significant features |
| Secondary Dev | Revisions, bug fixes, and the quick work that keeps things moving |

Freelancers slot into pod roles on a deliverables basis when capacity needs it. They follow the same accountability rules as the permanent team members they sit alongside.
