We rarely send formal proposals. Most clients close casually, founder-to-founder. When a written proposal is needed, it's generated from Launchpad and kept tight.

## When we send one

A proposal goes out when:

- The client requested one and won't move forward without it.
- The deal is large or complex enough that putting scope and pricing on paper helps everyone.
- An internal stakeholder on the client side needs something to approve against.

For most ecom founder deals, a WhatsApp summary and a Calendly link is the close.

## Format

Proposals are generated from **Launchpad's proposal tool**. Enter the client details and the scope; out comes a branded HTML proposal at a shareable URL.

Template-driven for speed, but every proposal is bespoke per client. The data, the numbers, and the projected lift are all specific to them.

## Structure

Standard sections, in order:

1. **The problem** — what we heard, what we observed.
2. **The approach** — the Conversion Engine framework as applied to this client.
3. **Scope** — what we deliver, what we don't.
4. **Pricing** — retainer tier and what comes with it.
5. **Next steps** — how to sign, when we start.

## What we deliberately leave out

We don't include the audit-level detail. The proposal sells the partnership; the audit (paid or in week 1) is what proves we can actually do the work.
