The audit is our wedge. It's the thing a prospect pays for (or is given) that proves we know what we're talking about before they sign onto a retainer.

## What the audit delivers

The output is **insight, presented**. Branded documentation, often a slide deck, driven by the Strategist's findings on the brand's funnel.

The audit is deliberately not as deep as the audit we run in **week 1 of a signed retainer**. The retainer audit is a selling point itself. If we hand it all over up front, we've given away the asset.

## What we look at

- **Position in the niche** — how the brand sits versus benchmarks for its category.
- **Funnel benchmarks** — where the brand should be, where it actually is.
- **Deeper analytics** — leaks in the funnel, drop-off points, the highest-leverage problems.

The **Strategist runs the audit**. They decide what to focus on based on what the brand actually needs, not a fixed template.

## Turnaround

**Target: 72 hours** from request to delivery. Faster shows we're operating at the pace we promise; slower undercuts the pitch.

## Paid, free, or included

- **Paid by default** — most audits are billed, because they take real Strategist time and signal we value the work.
- **Free** — sometimes for a client we particularly want, the audit itself is the wedge into a relationship we'd take a small loss to open.
- **Included** — week 1 of any signed Conversion Engine retainer includes a full, deeper audit.
