How a cold lead becomes a paying client. The whole arc, kept casual on purpose because that's how ecom founders actually buy.

## First touch

Leads land in one of three ways:

- **Inbound** — content, organic, direct. Goes to WhatsApp.
- **Referral** — straight to WhatsApp.
- **Cold ad traffic** — hits a landing page, completes a qualification questionnaire, then either books a call or drops into WhatsApp.

WhatsApp is the default channel because founders use it, it's casual, and it removes the friction of formal email back-and-forth.

## Qualifying

Cold traffic qualifies in at **$250K+ in annual revenue**. Below that and the Conversion Engine isn't the right offer for them.

Inbound and referrals don't have the same hard gate. There's usually enough context to know early.

## Discovery call

The first real conversation is a **30-minute diagnostic call**, run by **Dylan or Ajay**.

The goal isn't to pitch. It's to understand:

- How the funnel sits today
- Where the biggest leverage point is
- Whether we'd recommend a sprint on a few pages, or pitch the full retainer

Half the value of this call to the prospect is the clarity itself, even if they don't end up signing.

## What happens next

If there's a fit, we move toward either:

- A **paid audit** that opens the door to a retainer, or
- A **free audit included in week 1** of a signed Conversion Engine retainer

Audits are paid by default. The exception is when we want a particular client enough that the free audit is worth the trade.

## Closing

Formal proposals are rare. Ecom founders prefer casual founder-to-founder conversations, so the close usually happens in WhatsApp or in a short follow-up call. When a written proposal is needed, it gets generated from Launchpad (see Proposals).
