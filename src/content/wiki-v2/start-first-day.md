Welcome. Here's what to expect on day one and the first month, in the order it'll happen.

## Day one — setup

Get onto the tools you'll actually use:

- **Slack** — accept invite, get into the right channels (your pod, your discipline channel, `#team`).
- **Launchpad** — log in, set up your profile, get familiar with the dashboard.
- **Shopify** — Partner account access if you're on the dev or design side.
- **Figma** — workspace access, library access.

What you need depends on your role. Dylan will sort the right combination as part of onboarding.

## Onboarding POC

**Dylan is your point of contact for the first 30 days.** Use him to learn how Ecomlanders works, who the clients are, how we approach engagements, and anything that isn't yet documented. Asking is faster than guessing.

## Your first real task

Lower-priority deliverables in your role. Smaller projects, secondary work, things where the cost of a learning curve is low. Reading the wiki end to end is also genuinely useful for getting up to speed.

## What good looks like

**Week 1.** Understand the role and our offer in detail. By the end of week 1 you should be able to explain in your own words what we do and why someone hires us.

**First month.** Some competency in the role. Handle **70% of a deliverable** without help. Be proactive about learning. Show willingness to grow.

> **No pressure to be perfect in month one. Direction of travel matters more than a perfect hit from day one.**

If you're getting better each week and asking the right questions, you're doing it right.
