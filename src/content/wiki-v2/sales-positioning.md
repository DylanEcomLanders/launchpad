How we talk about what we sell. Use the language. Don't paraphrase.

## Core positioning

> **Ecomlanders is the conversion partner for DTC brands. We don't build pages. We build revenue systems.**

That's the one-liner. Memorise it.

## The conversion layer

Every serious DTC brand has three layers:

1. **Ad layer** — Meta agency, Google agency, TikTok agency. Drives traffic.
2. **Email layer** — Klaviyo agency. Captures and nurtures.
3. **Conversion layer** — **us**. Turns traffic into revenue.

Most brands have layers 1 and 2 dialled. Layer 3 is either missing or run by a freelancer doing one-off page builds. That's the gap. We are the conversion layer.

This positions us as essential infrastructure, not optional. You wouldn't run a DTC brand without an ad agency. You shouldn't run one without a conversion partner.

## The revenue anchor

Stop selling what's included. Sell what the CVR gap is costing them.

> Brand doing £800K/month. We lift site-wide CVR by 2%. That's £16K/month in recovered revenue. The partnership is £8K. They're paying half the value we deliver, and it compounds every month.

Frame every pricing conversation around this maths:

- **Their number** — monthly revenue and traffic (from SimilarWeb or conversation)
- **The gap** — current CVR vs benchmark (from the Conversion Matrix)
- **The cost of doing nothing** — revenue gap × 12 months
- **The partnership** — a fraction of the recovered revenue

The retainer isn't an expense. It's the cheapest way to capture revenue they're already earning but losing after the click.

## Premium price language

At £8K+/month the language has to match the positioning.

**Don't apologise for the price. Contextualise it.**

- "That's less than one senior CRO hire, and you get the full team."
- "Your ad spend is £50K/month. This is 16% of that, and it makes the other 84% work harder."
- "At a 0.1% CVR lift this pays for itself. We average 1-2% in 90 days."
- "Every month you delay costs roughly £X in lost revenue."

**Don't discount. Reframe.**

If someone pushes on price, don't drop it. Show the maths again. If the maths doesn't justify the retainer for their brand, they're not the right client. Walk away. The brands where the maths works will pay it without negotiating.

## Elevator pitches

**10 seconds:** "We're the conversion layer for DTC brands. You drive the traffic, we make sure it converts."

**30 seconds:** "Most DTC brands spend £50-100K/month driving traffic to pages that don't convert. We plug in as their conversion partner. Audit the funnel, own the roadmap, build the pages, run the tests, compound the results. Same ad spend, significantly more revenue."

**60 seconds (for prospects):** "Here's what we see with most brands doing £80K+/month. They've figured out the traffic side. Meta's working, Google's working, maybe TikTok's getting traction. But when someone clicks the ad, they land on a generic collection page or a PDP that hasn't been touched in 18 months. That's the conversion layer, and it's usually the weakest part of the business. We come in as the full conversion team. Audit every layer from landing through post-purchase, build a 90-day roadmap, then own execution: new pages, tests, AOV optimisation, post-purchase flows. We control the roadmap, you approve the direction. It compounds. Month over month your CVR climbs, your ROAS improves, your CAC drops. Most clients see the partnership pay for itself within 60 days."

## The dropshipper-to-brand bridge

A specific angle that lands hard for one segment:

> "There's a gap between running a dropshipping store and being a real brand. You've got the traffic, you've got the product-market fit, you're doing revenue. But your site looks like 2019, your pages don't convert, and your customers don't come back. We're the bridge. We take the post-click experience from dropshipper-grade to brand-grade, and the CVR and AOV follow."

This resonates because:

- They know their site is weak
- They can afford £8K/month (they're spending more on ads)
- They want to look and feel like the brands they're competing with
- The ROI is usually massive because their baseline CVR is so low

## The vendor-jumping argument

Most brands in our target range have used 2-3 agencies or freelancers in the last 18 months. Each switch:

- New onboarding, new context dump, new learning curve
- Previous work gets abandoned or half-implemented
- No compounding. Every project starts from scratch
- 3 months of momentum lost at every switch

This is the single biggest argument for the partnership model. We don't restart. We compound. Month 3 builds on Month 2. The data from the first test informs the next build. A new vendor can't do that. They're always starting cold.

**Use this on every sales call when they say "we've used agencies before."** It reframes the partnership as the solution to a problem they already feel deeply.
