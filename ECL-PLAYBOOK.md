After launch, track each asset. Measure against hypothesis. Worked → iterate. Didn't → diagnose why, test alternative. This is the compounding engine.

## 10. CONVERSION MATRIX — 8-LAYER SCORING

Score each layer 1-10. Below 6 = leaking. Those get prioritised.

### Layer 1: Traffic Quality
- Source diversity, audience-message match, device split, bounce by source

### Layer 2: Ad-to-Page Alignment
- Landing page matches ad? Scent trail consistency? Dedicated post-click pages?

### Layer 3: Landing Page / Homepage
- Value prop above fold, social proof visible, single CTA path, page speed <3s, mobile experience

### Layer 4: Product Detail Page (PDP)
- Image quality (4-6+), reviews near ATC, clear pricing, benefit-focused copy, sticky CTA mobile, trust signals

### Layer 5: Cart
- Cart-to-checkout rate (benchmark 55-65%), upsells limited, express checkout, free shipping bar, promo field hidden

### Layer 6: Checkout
- Checkout CVR (benchmark 28-35%), guest checkout, express pay, BNPL, field count minimised, trust near pay button

### Layer 7: Post-Purchase
- Thank-you page upsell, review request timed to delivery, email flows, unboxing experience

### Layer 8: Retention
- Email/SMS capture, repeat purchase rate, subscription offering, loyalty programme

### Interpreting Results
- **7+ average:** Strong. Hard to justify retainer unless specific layers weak.
- **5-7 average:** Good opportunity. Clear improvement path. Ideal client.
- **Below 5:** Significant gaps. High impact but may need foundational work.

## 11. REVENUE PROJECTOR

### The Formula
```
Monthly Revenue Gap = Monthly Traffic x (Benchmark CVR - Current CVR) x AOV
```

Example: 150,000 sessions x (3.2% - 1.8%) x £55 = **£115,500/mo left on the table**

### Breakeven Calculation
```
Breakeven CVR Lift = Retainer Cost / (Monthly Traffic x AOV)
```

Example: £9,000 / (150,000 x £55) = **0.11% CVR lift**

"The retainer pays for itself if we improve CVR by 0.11%. Our average client sees 1-2% in 90 days."

## 12. THE FUNNEL — 10-LAYER ARCHITECTURE

The funnel is not a linear pipeline. It's a pressure system. Every layer either builds momentum or bleeds it. When a layer fails, it degrades everything downstream.

### The Metrics Stack

| Stage | Metric | Benchmark |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| Sessions | Qualified sessions | Varies |
| Bounce Rate | Single-page sessions | 35-55% paid, 25-40% organic |
| PDP Views | Sessions with product view | 40-60% of sessions |
| Add to Cart | ATC rate from PDP | 8-12% |
| Cart View | Cart view rate | 65-80% of ATC |
| Checkout Init | Checkout rate from ATC | 55-65% |
| Checkout Done | Purchase from checkout | 28-35% |
| Overall CVR | Purchase / sessions | 1.5-3.5% cold, 3-6% warm |
| AOV | Revenue / orders | Category-dependent |
| Revenue/Session | Revenue / sessions | £2-8 DTC avg |

### Critical Connections

**Traffic → Everything:** Cold TikTok traffic behaves completely differently from warm email subscribers. Traffic source changes the benchmark for every metric downstream.

**Ad → Landing Page (The Handoff):** #1 cause of high-CPC, low-CVR campaigns. Lifestyle ad + product-on-white hero = broken conversation. 20-40% of traffic lost in first 3 seconds.

**Landing Page → PDP (The Bridge):** LP job is NOT to sell. It's to get the right product in front of the right person. 200 products + no guidance = asking cold traffic to do your merchandising.

**PDP → Cart (The Commitment):** ATC is the first commitment. If PDP shows free shipping but cart reveals £4.99 fee = trust destroyed at worst moment.

**Cart → Checkout (The Leak):** 35-45% of would-be customers lost here. Unexpected costs, forced accounts, complicated nav, too many upsells.

**Checkout → Post-Purchase (The Momentum):** Smooth checkout = dopamine hit. Thank-you page and post-purchase sequence must capitalise. Customer most receptive to upsells, reviews, referrals.

**Post-Purchase → Retention (The Loop):** First 7 days after purchase determine one-time buyer vs repeat customer. Delivery speed, unboxing, first email sequence = emotional foundation for LTV.

### The Economics
Brand doing £1M/year, 500K sessions, 2% CVR, £100 AOV:
- CVR 2% → 2.5%: +£250K/year (no extra ad spend)
- AOV £100 → £115: +£150K/year
- Both: +£437.5K/year (43.75% growth)

CRO is the only growth lever that compounds. Every improvement benefits every future session.

## 17. KEY TERMS — QUICK REFERENCE

**CVR** — Conversion rate. Purchase / sessions. 1.5-3.5% cold paid, 3-6% warm.
**AOV** — Average order value. Revenue / orders.
**RPS** — Revenue per session. The single best health metric.
**ATC** — Add to cart rate from PDP. Benchmark 8-12%.
**LTV** — Lifetime value. Calculate by cohort not blended.
**CAC** — Customer acquisition cost. All-in cost to acquire one customer.
**LTV:CAC** — Healthy DTC: 3:1+. Below 2:1 is unsustainable.
**ROAS** — Revenue per £1 ad spend. Breakeven depends on margin.
**ICE** — Impact x Confidence x Ease. Test prioritisation. Each 1-10.
**CTA** — Call to action. Primary (ATC) vs secondary (Learn More).
**UGC** — User-generated content. More trusted than brand content for cold traffic.
**BNPL** — Buy now pay later. Increases CVR 10-20% for orders over £50.
**RFM** — Recency, Frequency, Monetary. Customer segmentation model.
**CAPI** — Conversions API. Server-side tracking. Essential post-iOS 14.5.
**LCP** — Largest Contentful Paint. Target <2.5s. Primary speed metric.
